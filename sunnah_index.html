What Is Sunnah?

سنت کا معنی ہے نبی ﷺ کا طریقہ — ان کے الفاظ، اعمال اور جن باتوں کو آپ نے منظور کیا۔ یہ مسلمانوں کی عملی زندگی کے لیے ایک زندہ رہنمائی ہے۔

یہ ماڈیول آپ ﷺ کے روزمرہ کے ذاتی اعمال پر مرکوز ہے — بیدار ہونے سے تہجد تک، وضو سے دسترخوان تک، ہر چھوٹے عمل سے ذاتی تعلیمات تک۔

یہاں موجود ہر عمل مستند ہے — صرف صحیح یا حسن درجے کی احادیث۔ ہر حدیث کا نمبر موجود ہے۔ کوئی بھی عمل نمبر کے بغیر شامل نہیں۔

The Sunnah is the way of the Prophet ﷺ — his words, his actions, and what he approved. It is not merely history; it is a living template that has been preserved with extraordinary precision across fourteen centuries.

This module covers his daily personal practices across five modules: from the moment he woke to the moment he lay down again, from his precise physical movements to the private duas he taught those he loved most. Everything here is authenticated — Sahih or Hasan-graded hadith only. Every practice carries a hadith number. Nothing is included without one.

Three authenticity tiers are used throughout:

●✦ Confirmed SunnahSahih Bukhari or Muslim · Highest grade
●◈ Established SunnahAbu Dawud / Tirmidhi / Ibn Majah · Sahih or Hasan Sahih
●◇ Reported PracticeHasan chain only · Always clearly labelled

A Note on Authenticity

This module contains only authenticated practices. Nothing is included without a hadith number from a named primary collection. If a practice appears widely in popular Islamic content but cannot be traced to a graded narration, it is not here.

Daily Sunnah claims carry the highest responsibility in Islamic knowledge — asserting that the Prophet ﷺ did something regularly is a weighty claim that demands precision. The strictest standard is applied throughout: Tier 1 (Bukhari/Muslim), Tier 2 (other collections, Sahih or Hasan Sahih), Tier 3 (Hasan — always amber-badged and explicitly distinguished). Where scholars differ on grading or interpretation, the difference is noted.

The goal is not completeness of every possible practice — it is reliability of every included practice.
