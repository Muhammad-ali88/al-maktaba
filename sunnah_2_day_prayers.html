◈ Hasan Sahih — Tirmidhi / Muslim ☀️ Every Encounter PRACTICE 11

The Salam — How He Greeted

السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ وَرَحْمَةُ اللَّهِ وَبَرَكَاتُهُ
Assalamu alaykum wa rahmatullahi wa barakatuh
Peace be upon you, and the mercy of Allah, and His blessings.
The Reward of the Full Salam
وَعَلَيْكُمُ السَّلَامُ وَرَحْمَةُ اللَّهِ وَبَرَكَاتُهُ — ثَلَاثُونَ
A man passed by the Prophet ﷺ and said "Assalamu alaykum" — he was given ten. Another said "wa rahmatullah" — twenty. Another said the full greeting — he was given thirty. (Sunan Abu Dawud 5195; Sunan Tirmidhi 2689 — Hasan Sahih)

He ﷺ initiated salam — he never waited for others to greet him first. He greeted everyone: adults and children, the known and the unknown, the important and the ordinary. He would seek out children specifically and greet them, teaching his companions humility through the act of saluting those the world often overlooked. Salam was not a social formality for him — it was a complete prayer: a supplication for the other person's peace, mercy, and blessing compressed into a single utterance. He said: "You will not enter Jannah until you believe, and you will not believe until you love one another. Shall I tell you something that, if you do it, you will love one another? Spread salam among yourselves." (Sahih Muslim 54)

آپ ﷺ خود سلام کی ابتداء کرتے — کبھی انتظار نہیں کرتے کہ دوسرے پہلے کہیں۔ آپ ہر کسی کو سلام کرتے: بڑوں کو، بچوں کو، جانے پہچانے کو اور انجان کو۔ آپ خاص طور پر بچوں کو سلام کرتے، اپنے صحابہ کو عاجزی سکھانے کے لیے۔ آپ نے فرمایا: "تم جنت میں داخل نہیں ہوگے جب تک ایمان نہ لاؤ، اور ایمان نہ لاؤ گے جب تک آپس میں محبت نہ کرو۔ کیا میں تمہیں وہ چیز نہ بتاؤں جس سے تم آپس میں محبت کرنے لگو گے؟ سلام کو عام کرو۔" (صحیح مسلم 54)

📚 Sunan Tirmidhi, Book of Seeking Permission (Kitab al-Isti'dhan), Hadith 2721 — Hasan Sahih; Sahih Muslim, Book of Faith (Kitab al-Iman), Hadith 54 — Sahih; Greeted children: Sahih Bukhari, Book of Good Manners, Hadith 6247 — Sahih; Reward of full salam: Tirmidhi 2689, Abu Dawud 5195 — Hasan Sahih
◈ Why This Matters Salam costs nothing and changes everything about how an interaction begins. He ﷺ made the first moment of every encounter a prayer — a blessing offered before any request, task, or business was discussed.
🌿 Try Today Initiate salam today — don't wait to be greeted first. Use the full greeting: Assalamu alaykum wa rahmatullahi wa barakatuh. Give all thirty.
◇ Hasan — Tirmidhi ☀️ Every Interaction PRACTICE 12

His Smile — The Charity He Gave Everyone

تَبَسُّمُكَ فِي وَجْهِ أَخِيكَ صَدَقَةٌ
Tabassmuka fi wajhi akhika sadaqah
"Your smile in the face of your brother is charity."

Abdullah ibn al-Harith RA said: "I never saw anyone who smiled more than the Prophet ﷺ." His smile was constant — not a performance, not a mask, but his natural state in the presence of people. He rarely laughed out loud, and when he did, those present said they could see his blessed teeth. But the smile — warm, present, unhurried — was always there. He would turn his full body toward whoever he was speaking with, give them his complete attention, and they would leave feeling they were the most important person he had met that day. His smile was the beginning of that gift. It was not charm; it was sincerity.

عبداللہ بن حارث رضی اللہ عنہ نے فرمایا: "میں نے نبی ﷺ سے زیادہ مسکرانے والا کوئی نہیں دیکھا۔" آپ کی مسکراہٹ مستقل تھی — کوئی دکھاوا نہیں، کوئی نقاب نہیں، بلکہ آپ کی فطری حالت۔ آپ بلند آواز سے کم ہنستے، اور جب ہنستے تو لوگ آپ کے دانت دیکھتے۔ آپ جس سے بھی گفتگو کرتے، پوری توجہ دیتے، پورا جسم اس کی طرف موڑتے۔ لوگ آپ سے ملنے کے بعد یہ محسوس کرتے کہ وہ سب سے اہم انسان تھے۔

📚 "Smile is charity": Sunan Tirmidhi, Book of Virtuous Conduct (Kitab al-Birr), Hadith 1956 — Hasan; His character of smiling: Sunan Tirmidhi, Hadith 3641 — Sahih
◈ Why This Matters He made everyone he spoke with feel they were the most important person. The smile was the beginning of that — a simple, costless act that declared: you matter, I see you, I am glad you exist.
🌿 Try Today Smile consciously at the first three people you interact with today. Not a polite gesture — a genuine, deliberate smile that says: I see you.
✦ Sahih — Bukhari & Muslim 🍽️ At Every Meal PRACTICE 13

He Never Criticised Food

مَا عَابَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ طَعَامًا قَطُّ إِنِ اشْتَهَاهُ أَكَلَهُ وَإِنْ كَرِهَهُ تَرَكَهُ
Ma 'aba-n-nabiyyu ﷺ ta'aman qattu — in ishtahahu akalahu wa in karihahu tarakahu
"The Prophet ﷺ never criticised food. If he desired it, he ate it. If he disliked it, he left it." — Narrated by Aisha RA

This was one of Aisha's RA most consistent descriptions of his character at the table. If he liked what was presented, he ate — with gratitude, with bismillah, without complaint. If he disliked a particular food, he simply left it on the plate without comment, without a look of displeasure, without a word that might make the host feel bad. He never complained about how food tasted, how it was prepared, whether it was too salty or too bland, too hot or too cold. The host's effort was always honoured. Gratitude was the reflex, not criticism. This was both humility and consideration — the table was never the site of his displeasure.

یہ حضرت عائشہ رضی اللہ عنہا کی آپ ﷺ کے دسترخوان پر کردار کی سب سے مستقل تعریفوں میں سے ایک ہے۔ اگر کھانا پسند آیا تو کھایا — شکرگزاری کے ساتھ، بسم اللہ کے ساتھ۔ اگر ناپسند ہوا تو بس چھوڑ دیا — بغیر کسی تبصرے کے، بغیر ناگواری کے اظہار کے۔ آپ نے کبھی کھانے کے ذائقے، پکانے کے طریقے یا نمک مرچ کی شکایت نہیں کی۔ میزبان کی محنت ہمیشہ عزت کی نگاہ سے دیکھی جاتی تھی۔ شکر ان کا پہلا ردعمل تھا۔

📚 Sahih Bukhari, Book of Food (Kitab al-At'imah), Hadith 5409; Sahih Muslim, Book of Drinks (Kitab al-Ashriba), Hadith 2064 — Sahih
◈ Why This Matters Criticising food is a form of ingratitude — to the host who prepared it and to Allah who provided it. He ﷺ never used food as a domain of complaint, even in private.
🌿 Try Today No complaints about food today — not aloud, not in your expression. Whatever you eat: eat it with gratitude. If you dislike something, simply leave it without comment.
◈ Hasan Sahih — Ibn Hibban / Ibn Majah 🌤️ After Dhuhr PRACTICE 14

Qailulah — The Midday Rest

The Prophet ﷺ would rest briefly in the early afternoon — a short, intentional pause between the morning's work and the afternoon ahead. The Arabic term qailulah refers specifically to this midday rest, distinguished in Islamic tradition from the longer night sleep. He did not treat ceaseless activity as a virtue. He understood that the body has a natural rhythm, that tiredness is real, and that rest — when taken deliberately — is part of productive worship, not opposed to it. By resting, he could return to his afternoon and evening duties with renewed clarity and energy. Modern sleep science calls this a power nap; he simply called it sunnah.

نبی ﷺ دوپہر میں مختصر آرام کرتے — صبح کے کاموں اور سہ پہر کے درمیان ایک مختصر، ارادی وقفہ۔ قیلولہ — یہ عربی اصطلاح اسی دوپہر کے آرام کے لیے مخصوص ہے۔ آپ مسلسل مصروفیت کو تقویٰ نہیں سمجھتے تھے۔ آپ جانتے تھے کہ جسم کی ایک فطری لے ہے اور آرام اگر ارادے کے ساتھ لیا جائے تو عبادت کا حصہ ہے۔ آرام کے بعد آپ اپنی سہ پہر اور شام کی ذمہ داریوں میں تازگی کے ساتھ واپس آتے۔

📋 Scholarly Note This practice is documented in Ibn Hibban (Hadith 2737) and Sunan Ibn Majah (Hadith 1693), authenticated by Imam al-Albani as Sahih and Hasan respectively. It is a recommended (mustahabb) practice, not obligatory. The narration "Take qailulah, for the shayateen do not take naps" is reported with this grading and should not be attributed to a stronger classification.
📚 Ibn Hibban, Hadith 2737 — authenticated by Imam al-Albani: Sahih; Sunan Ibn Majah, Book of Establishing Prayer (Kitab Iqamat al-Salah), Hadith 1693 — Hasan
◈ Why This Matters He did not equate busyness with piety. His daily structure included rest as a deliberate element — not laziness, not wasted time, but intentional recovery that served every act of worship that followed.
🌿 Try Today Even 10–15 minutes of rest after Dhuhr — eyes closed, phone face-down, nothing playing. Let the afternoon begin from a place of rest, not exhaustion.
◈ Hasan — Abu Dawud 🏠 Arriving Home PRACTICE 15

Entering the Home — The Dua of Return

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ وَلَجْنَا وَبِسْمِ اللَّهِ خَرَجْنَا وَعَلَى اللَّهِ رَبِّنَا تَوَكَّلْنَا
Bismillahi walajna, wa bismillahi kharajna, wa 'ala Allahi rabbina tawakkalna
In the name of Allah we enter, in the name of Allah we leave, and upon Allah our Lord we rely.

When the Prophet ﷺ returned home, he entered with bismillah and greeted whoever was inside with salam. The home, for him, was not merely a building — it was a sanctuary, and entering it was a sacred act. He taught that when a person enters their home with bismillah and salam, the shaytaan says "there is no place for me here tonight." The home was spiritually set when entered correctly. He greeted his family warmly upon arrival — not absorbed in tasks, not distant — present. Aisha RA described how he would enter and immediately engage with his household. The threshold was not crossed unconsciously.

جب نبی ﷺ گھر واپس آتے تو بسم اللہ کے ساتھ داخل ہوتے اور گھر میں موجود سب کو سلام کرتے۔ گھر ان کے لیے محض ایک عمارت نہیں تھی — یہ ایک پناہ گاہ تھی اور اس میں داخل ہونا ایک مقدس عمل۔ آپ نے سکھایا کہ جب کوئی بسم اللہ اور سلام کے ساتھ گھر میں داخل ہو تو شیطان کہتا ہے: "آج رات میرے لیے یہاں جگہ نہیں۔" حضرت عائشہ رضی اللہ عنہا نے بتایا کہ آپ ﷺ گھر میں آتے ہی فوری طور پر اہل خانہ سے مل کر بیٹھتے۔

📚 Sunan Abu Dawud, Book of Good Manners (Kitab al-Adab), Hadith 5096 — Hasan (graded by scholars of hadith); Greeting family on entry: Quran 24:61; Shaytaan leaves: Sunan Ibn Majah, Hadith 3887 — Hasan
◈ Why This Matters How you enter your home sets the spiritual atmosphere of the entire space. The dua is short, the salam is easy — but together they transform an ordinary act of returning into an act of tawakkul and blessing.
🌿 Try Today Say this dua at your front door as you step inside. Then greet your family with salam — don't walk past them. Make the return home a conscious act of presence.

The Rawatib Sunnah — The Twelve Rakats

مَنْ صَلَّى اثْنَتَيْ عَشْرَةَ رَكْعَةً فِي يَوْمٍ وَلَيْلَةٍ بَنَى اللَّهُ لَهُ بَيْتًا فِي الْجَنَّةِ
Man salla ithnatay 'ashrata rak'atan fi yawmin wa laylatin bana Allahu lahu baytan fi-l-jannah
"Whoever prays twelve rakats in the day and night, Allah will build for him a house in Paradise."

📋 Honest Scholarly Note — Asr Rawatib There is no specifically confirmed rawatib sunnah before Asr in the hadith of the twelve rakats. Some scholars recommend voluntary prayers before Asr based on the general hadith "Allah have mercy on the one who prays four before Asr" (Abu Dawud 1271 — Hasan), but this is not the same as the established rawatib. The twelve-rakat hadith does not include Asr. This distinction matters and should be preserved.

These twelve rakats were the architecture of his ﷺ day — voluntary prayers that bracketed each obligatory prayer, creating a rhythm of return to Allah throughout the day's activities. They were not random extra prayers but a specific, named pattern he taught and consistently maintained. Aisha RA and Umm Habiba RA were among the primary narrators of this practice. He described the reward in the most concrete terms available: a house in Jannah. Not a vague reward, not a general merit — a house, built by Allah, for anyone who maintains this practice every day of their life.

یہ بارہ رکعتیں آپ ﷺ کے دن کا ڈھانچہ تھیں — نفل نمازیں جو ہر فرض نماز کے گرد لپٹی ہوئی تھیں، پورے دن میں اللہ کی طرف واپسی کی لے پیدا کرتی تھیں۔ یہ بے ترتیب نمازیں نہیں بلکہ ایک مخصوص، نامزد طریقہ تھا جو آپ نے سکھایا اور مستقل برقرار رکھا۔ آپ نے اس کا اجر سب سے ٹھوس الفاظ میں بیان فرمایا: جنت میں ایک گھر — کوئی مبہم ثواب نہیں، بلکہ ایک گھر جو اللہ خود بناتا ہے۔

📚 Sahih Muslim, Book of the Prayer of Travellers (Kitab Salat al-Musafirin), Hadith 728; Sunan Tirmidhi, Book of Prayer (Kitab al-Salah), Hadith 428 — Sahih
◈ Why This Matters A house in Jannah for twelve daily rakats — this is the most specific reward mentioned for any daily sunnah practice. He ﷺ told us the price. He also showed us the product every single day.
🌿 Try Today Start with two before Fajr (already in Part 1) and two after Dhuhr. That is four. Build from there — one prayer at a time, until the twelve become your daily architecture too.
✦ Sahih — Muslim ☀️ After Sunrise — Before Dhuhr PRACTICE 17

Duha Prayer — The Prayer of the Forenoon

يُصْبِحُ عَلَى كُلِّ سُلَامَى مِنَ أَحَدِكُمْ صَدَقَةٌ وَيُجْزِئُ مِنْ ذَلِكَ رَكْعَتَانِ يَرْكَعُهُمَا مِنَ الضُّحَى
Yusbahu 'ala kulli sulamy min ahadikum sadaqatun, wa yujzi'u min dhalika rak'atani yarka'uhuma mina-d-duha
"Every morning a charity is due on every joint of your body. And two rakats of Duha will suffice for all of that."

Abu Hurairah RA narrated that his close friend the Prophet ﷺ advised him three things he never abandoned: fasting three days of every month, praying two rakats of Duha, and praying Witr before sleeping. The Duha prayer — prayed after the sun has fully risen until just before midday — is the prayer of the morning light, offered when the world is already in motion. Every morning, 360 good deeds are due from a person — one for each joint in the human body — as a form of gratitude for the body that serves them. Two rakats of Duha clear this entire account. He ﷺ was sometimes seen praying eight rakats of Duha (Sahih Muslim 719). The minimum is two. The maximum has no upper limit set, though he generally prayed more.

حضرت ابوہریرہ رضی اللہ عنہ نے بتایا کہ ان کے خلیل نبی ﷺ نے انہیں تین باتوں کی وصیت فرمائی جو انہوں نے کبھی نہیں چھوڑی: ہر مہینے تین دن روزہ، دو رکعت چاشت، اور سونے سے پہلے وتر۔ چاشت کی نماز — سورج اچھی طرح طلوع ہونے کے بعد سے ظہر سے پہلے تک — صبح کے نور میں ادا کی جاتی ہے جب دنیا پہلے سے حرکت میں آ چکی ہوتی ہے۔ ہر صبح انسان کے جسم کے ہر جوڑ پر صدقہ واجب ہے — 360 جوڑ، 360 نیکیاں۔ چاشت کی دو رکعت یہ سارا حساب ادا کر دیتی ہیں۔

📚 Sahih Muslim, Book of the Prayer of Travellers (Kitab Salat al-Musafirin), Hadith 716 (Abu Hurairah's three acts); 360 joints hadith: Sahih Muslim, Hadith 720; Eight rakats: Sahih Muslim, Hadith 719 — Sahih
◈ Why This Matters While the world transacts, the believer pauses to acknowledge the Giver of the morning. Two rakats of Duha settle the entire morning's account of gratitude — and take less than five minutes.
🌿 Try Today Pray two rakats of Duha today — after the sun has fully risen, before Dhuhr. Find a quiet space, even briefly. It does not require a mosque. Let this be your morning's gratitude offering.
✦ Sahih — Bukhari & Muslim 🕌 In Every Prayer PRACTICE 18

His Focus in Prayer — The Way He Prayed

His prayer was not a physical routine to be completed — it was an audience with Allah, entered with full consciousness and left without haste. Four dimensions of his prayer practice are authenticated across the hadith collections:

آپ ﷺ کی نماز محض ادا کرنے کا معمول نہیں تھی — یہ اللہ کے ساتھ ملاقات تھی، پوری شعوری حاضری کے ساتھ داخل ہونا اور بغیر جلدی کے نکلنا۔ آپ کی نماز کے چار پہلو حدیث کی کتابوں میں مستند ہیں:

18a — Khushu: Presence and Focus · الخشوع
أَنْ تَعْبُدَ اللَّهَ كَأَنَّكَ تَرَاهُ فَإِنْ لَمْ تَكُنْ تَرَاهُ فَإِنَّهُ يَرَاكَ
An ta'buda Allaha ka-annaka tarahu, fa-in lam takun tarahu fa-innahu yaraka
"Worship Allah as though you see Him, and if you cannot see Him, know that He sees you." — Definition of Ihsan (excellence in worship)
📚 Sahih Bukhari, Book of Faith (Kitab al-Iman), Hadith 50 — Sahih | Quran 23:1–2
18b — He Prayed Slowly · الطمأنينة
ارْجِعْ فَصَلِّ فَإِنَّكَ لَمْ تُصَلِّ
Irji' fa-salli, fa-innaka lam tusalli
"Go back and pray, for you have not prayed." — Said to a man who prayed quickly without settling in each position
📚 Sahih Bukhari, Book of Prayer (Kitab al-Salah), Hadith 779 — Sahih
18c — Dua in Sujood · الدعاء في السجود
أَقْرَبُ مَا يَكُونُ الْعَبْدُ مِنْ رَبِّهِ وَهُوَ سَاجِدٌ فَأَكْثِرُوا الدُّعَاءَ
Aqrabu ma yakunu-l-'abdu min rabbihi wa huwa sajidun — fa-akthiru-d-du'a'
"The closest a servant is to his Lord is when he is in prostration — so make much dua."
📚 Sahih Muslim, Book of Prayer (Kitab al-Salah), Hadith 482 — Sahih
18d — He Prayed Asr Consistently · المحافظة على العصر
مَنْ فَاتَتْهُ صَلَاةُ الْعَصْرِ فَكَأَنَّمَا وُتِرَ أَهْلَهُ وَمَالَهُ
Man fatat-hu salatu-l-'asri fa-ka-annama wutira ahlahu wa malah
"Whoever misses Asr, it is as if he has lost his family and his wealth." — The gravity of this prayer specifically
📚 Sahih Bukhari, Book of the Times of Prayer (Kitab Mawaqit al-Salah), Hadith 552 — Sahih
📚 All four practices above sourced individually with hadith numbers. Combined they represent his authenticated prayer character from Bukhari and Muslim.
◈ Why This Matters He ﷺ rebuked a man who prayed quickly and told him he had not actually prayed. The physical motions without presence are not the prayer he taught. Sujood is the closest point to Allah available in this life — he said to use it.
🌿 Try Today In your next prayer: slow down in sujood. Make one sincere dua in your own words — in any language. Stay a moment longer than usual. Let the closest point to Allah not be rushed.
✦ Sahih — Bukhari & Muslim ☀️ Throughout the Day PRACTICE 19

Remembrance Between Prayers — Continuous Dhikr

سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ وَبِحَمْدِهِ — سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ الْعَظِيمِ
SubhanAllahi wa bihamdihi — SubhanAllahil 'Azeem
Glory be to Allah and praise Him — Glory be to Allah the Most Great.
The Lightest Words, the Heaviest Scales
"Two phrases are light on the tongue, heavy on the scales, beloved to the Most Merciful: SubhanAllahi wa bihamdihi, SubhanAllahil Azeem." (Sahih Bukhari, Book of Invocations, Hadith 6682 — Sahih)
One Thousand Good Deeds in Minutes
أَيَعْجِزُ أَحَدُكُمْ أَنْ يَكْسِبَ كُلَّ يَوْمٍ أَلْفَ حَسَنَةٍ
"Is any of you unable to earn a thousand good deeds each day? By saying SubhanAllah one hundred times, a thousand good deeds are written for him and a thousand sins are erased." (Sahih Muslim, Book of Dhikr, Hadith 2698 — Sahih)

He ﷺ was described by those closest to him as being in a constant state of dhikr — not as a performed ritual but as a living condition. His lips moved between conversations. He remembered Allah in the pauses, in the transitions, in the waiting. Aisha RA said his dhikr was at all times, not restricted to special moments. He would say SubhanAllah, Alhamdulillah, Allahu Akbar — spontaneously, without an occasion, because the presence of Allah was not something he accessed at scheduled times. It was his continuous state. The tongue, he taught, should always be moist with the remembrance of Allah — and the easiest of all is SubhanAllahi wa bihamdihi.

آپ ﷺ کے قریبی لوگوں نے بیان کیا کہ آپ ہمیشہ ذکر کی حالت میں رہتے — کوئی رسمی ادائیگی نہیں بلکہ ایک زندہ کیفیت۔ گفتگو کے درمیان آپ کے ہونٹ حرکت میں رہتے۔ آپ وقفوں میں، منتقلی کے لمحوں میں، انتظار میں اللہ کو یاد کرتے۔ حضرت عائشہ رضی اللہ عنہا نے فرمایا آپ کا ذکر ہر وقت ہوتا تھا۔ آپ نے سکھایا کہ زبان ہمیشہ اللہ کے ذکر سے تر رہنی چاہیے۔

📚 Sahih Bukhari, Book of Invocations (Kitab al-Da'awat), Hadith 6682; Sahih Muslim, Book of Dhikr and Dua (Kitab al-Dhikr), Hadith 2698; His continuous dhikr: Sunan Abu Dawud, Book of Prayer, Hadith 1316 — Sahih / Hasan
◈ Why This Matters Dhikr is available at every moment — standing, sitting, walking, waiting in traffic. There is no time or place too ordinary for it. The scales of deeds do not know the difference between a moment of prayer and a moment of dhikr in traffic.
🌿 Try Today Say SubhanAllahil Azeem 100 times today — in traffic, while waiting, during a walk. It takes three to four minutes and earns a thousand good deeds. Your tongue can do this while your hands do something else.

Period 7 — With People

مع الناس
◈ Hasan Sahih — Tirmidhi / Shamaa'il 🤝 In Every Conversation PRACTICE 20

How He Listened — Total Presence

He ﷺ gave every person he spoke with an experience that was difficult to forget: the feeling of being completely seen. When he met someone and shook their hand, he would not pull his hand away until the other person let go first. He would not turn his face away from someone until they turned first. He turned his entire body toward the person speaking to him — not just his face, but his full physical presence. He never interrupted. He waited until the person had fully expressed themselves. Anas RA narrated that if someone were to pull his hand away from the Prophet's grip, they could not — he held on until the other person was ready to release. These were not techniques. They were the natural overflow of how he felt about people.

آپ ﷺ نے ہر اس شخص کو جس سے بات کرتے، ایک ناقابل فراموش تجربہ دیا: مکمل طور پر دیکھے جانے کا احساس۔ جب آپ کسی سے مصافحہ کرتے تو اپنا ہاتھ اس وقت تک نہ کھینچتے جب تک دوسرا نہ کھینچے۔ آپ اپنا چہرہ اس وقت تک نہیں موڑتے جب تک دوسرا نہ موڑے۔ آپ اپنا پورا جسم بات کرنے والے کی طرف موڑتے — صرف چہرہ نہیں، پوری جسمانی حاضری۔ حضرت انس رضی اللہ عنہ نے بتایا کہ اگر کوئی آپ کے ہاتھ سے اپنا ہاتھ چھڑانا چاہتا تو نہیں چھڑا سکتا تھا۔

📚 Sunan Tirmidhi, Book of Virtuous Conduct (Kitab al-Birr), Hadith 2490 — Hasan Sahih; Shamaa'il Muhammadiyyah (al-Tirmidhi) — Hasan Sahih — descriptions of his physical presence in conversation
◈ Why This Matters In an age of constant distraction, his total presence was his greatest gift to every person. He had no phone to look at — but even so, he chose presence deliberately. We have to choose it against everything pulling us away.
🌿 Try Today In your next conversation — face the person fully. Put your phone face-down or in your pocket. Do not interrupt. Wait until they are genuinely finished before you speak. Give them the experience he gave everyone.
✦ Sahih — Bukhari 🤝 In All Conduct PRACTICE 21

He Never Made Fun of Anyone

لَا تُسَخِّرْ مِنْ أَخِيكَ فِي بَلِيَّتِهِ فَيَرْحَمَهُ اللَّهُ وَيَبْتَلِيَكَ
La tusakhkhir min akhika fi baliyyatihi fa-yarhamahu Allahu wa yabtalik
"Do not mock your brother in his affliction, lest Allah have mercy on him and afflict you with it."

He ﷺ never mocked anyone — not their appearance, not their mistakes, not their circumstances. He never ridiculed, never humiliated, never exposed anyone's weakness for the benefit of others in the room. When someone in his community made a mistake, he would address it without naming names: "What is wrong with some people who do..." — correcting the behaviour while protecting the person's dignity. Aisha RA narrated that he never took personal revenge for himself. He only responded when the sanctities of Allah were violated — and even then, his correction was proportionate, purposeful, never cruel. He understood the difference between correction and humiliation, and he would only ever do the former.

آپ ﷺ نے کبھی کسی کا مذاق نہیں اڑایا — نہ ان کی شکل کا، نہ ان کی غلطیوں کا، نہ ان کے حالات کا۔ آپ نے کبھی کسی کو ذلیل نہیں کیا اور نہ کسی کی کمزوری دوسروں کے سامنے ظاہر کی۔ جب کسی نے غلطی کی تو آپ نام لیے بغیر کہتے: "لوگوں کو کیا ہو گیا ہے جو ایسا کرتے ہیں..." — کردار کو درست کرتے ہوئے فرد کی عزت محفوظ رکھتے۔ حضرت عائشہ رضی اللہ عنہا نے فرمایا کہ آپ نے کبھی اپنی ذات کے لیے بدلہ نہیں لیا۔ آپ اصلاح اور تذلیل کا فرق جانتے تھے، اور آپ نے ہمیشہ صرف اصلاح کی۔

📚 Sahih Bukhari, Book of Good Manners (Kitab al-Adab), Hadith 6786 — Sahih; Hadith on not mocking in affliction: Sunan Tirmidhi, Book of Asceticism (Kitab al-Zuhd), Hadith 2506 — Hasan
◈ Why This Matters His correction was always aimed at the behaviour, never an attack on the person's dignity. This distinction — between correction and humiliation — is the difference between the sunnah and the opposite of it.
🌿 Try Today Notice how you correct people today — in speech, in tone, in expression. Ask: is this correction or humiliation? Is this sunnah or its opposite?
✦ Sahih — Bukhari & Muslim 🤝 Community Practice PRACTICE 22

Visiting the Sick — Two Duas of Healing

لَا بَأْسَ طَهُورٌ إِنْ شَاءَ اللَّهُ
La ba's, tahurun in sha Allah
No harm — a purification, if Allah wills.
اللَّهُمَّ رَبَّ النَّاسِ أَذْهِبِ الْبَأْسَ اشْفِهِ وَأَنْتَ الشَّافِي لَا شِفَاءَ إِلَّا شِفَاؤُكَ شِفَاءً لَا يُغَادِرُ سَقَمًا
Allahumma rabbannasi, adh-hibi-l-ba's, ishfihi wa anta-sh-shafi, la shifa'a illa shifa'uka, shifa'an la yughadiru saqama
O Allah, Lord of mankind — remove the harm, cure him/her. You are the Curer, there is no cure except Your cure — a cure that leaves no illness behind.

Visiting the sick was not an occasional charitable act for him ﷺ — it was a consistent, woven-in practice of community care. He would go personally. He would sit with the person, not just pass by. He visited companions, neighbours, and he famously visited the Jewish boy who had served him when he became ill — and offered him Islam as he sat with him at his deathbed. He said: "Whoever visits a sick Muslim in the morning, seventy thousand angels pray for him until evening. Whoever visits in the evening, seventy thousand angels pray for him until morning." (Sunan Tirmidhi 969 — Hasan). He also said the visitor remains in a garden of paradise until they return home. (Sahih Muslim 2568)

بیماروں کی عیادت آپ ﷺ کے لیے کوئی کبھی کبھار کا کام نہیں تھا — یہ برادری کی دیکھ بھال کا ایک مستقل، باقاعدہ حصہ تھا۔ آپ خود تشریف لے جاتے۔ آپ بیٹھتے — صرف گزر نہیں جاتے۔ آپ نے اس یہودی لڑکے کی بھی عیادت کی جو آپ کی خدمت کرتا تھا جب وہ بیمار ہوا۔ آپ نے فرمایا: "جو مسلمان صبح کسی بیمار کی عیادت کرے، ستر ہزار فرشتے شام تک اس کے لیے دعا کرتے ہیں۔"

📚 "La ba's tahurun": Sahih Bukhari, Book of Patients (Kitab al-Marda), Hadith 5656 — Sahih; Full shifa dua: Sahih Bukhari, Hadith 5675; Sahih Muslim, Book of Greetings, Hadith 2191 — Sahih; Garden of paradise: Sahih Muslim, Book of Greetings, Hadith 2568 — Sahih; Angels praying: Sunan Tirmidhi, Book of Funerals, Hadith 969 — Hasan
◈ Why This Matters Community care was woven into his daily life — not separated from his "religious" practices. Visiting the sick, for him, was as much an act of worship as any prayer. The person lying in hospital did not become less important because the world kept moving.
🌿 Try Today Think of one person who is sick, in hospital, or struggling. Send them a message today — or visit if possible. Say the dua for them — even in their absence. This is his practice.
