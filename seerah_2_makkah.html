01Fatrat al-Wahy — The Pause in Revelation · فترة الوحي
📅 ~610 CE · Shortly After First Revelation
📍 Makkah al-Mukarramah
فترة الوحي — ثم نزول سورة المدثر
The Silence — Then the Command to Arise

After the first revelation in Cave Hira, Jibril AS did not return immediately. A period of silence — Fatrat al-Wahy — ensued. Jabir ibn Abdullah RA narrates in Sahih Bukhari (4953) that the Prophet ﷺ said: "While I was walking I heard a voice from the sky and raised my eyes. Behold — the angel who had come to me in Hira was sitting on a throne between the sky and the earth. I was frightened of him and went home and said: 'Cover me! Cover me!' Then Allah revealed Surat al-Muddaththir." The Prophet ﷺ had been deeply distressed during the pause, and Khadijah RA had consoled him through it.

With Surat al-Muddaththir (74:1–5), the passive, frightened man covered in his blanket was called to become the active warner of all humanity. This was the transition from private experience to public mission. Revelation would continue now without interruption for the next twenty-two years.

پہلی وحی کے بعد وحی کا سلسلہ رک گیا — "فترۃ الوحی۔" صحیح بخاری ۴۹۵۳ میں جابر بن عبداللہ رضی اللہ عنہ روایت کرتے ہیں کہ آپ ﷺ نے فرمایا: "میں چل رہا تھا کہ آسمان سے آواز سنی — نظر اٹھائی تو وہی فرشتہ جو حرا میں آیا تھا آسمان اور زمین کے درمیان کرسی پر بیٹھا ہے۔ میں ڈر گیا، گھر آیا، کہا: مجھے ڈھانپو!" خدیجہ رضی اللہ عنہا نے اس دوران تسلی دی۔ پھر سورۃ المدثر نازل ہوئی — چادر میں لپٹے ہوئے کو حکم ملا: اٹھو اور ڈراؤ۔

📖 Quran · Surat al-Muddaththir (74:1–5) — Resumed After the Pause
يَا أَيُّهَا الْمُدَّثِّرُ ﴿١﴾ قُمْ فَأَنذِرْ ﴿٢﴾ وَرَبَّكَ فَكَبِّرْ ﴿٣﴾ وَثِيَابَكَ فَطَهِّرْ ﴿٤﴾ وَالرُّجْزَ فَاهْجُرْ ﴿٥﴾
"O you who covers himself — arise and warn. And your Lord glorify. And your clothing purify. And uncleanliness avoid." (Saheeh International · Surat al-Muddaththir 74:1–5)
"اے چادر اوڑھنے والے! اٹھو اور ڈراؤ۔ اور اپنے رب کی بڑائی بیان کرو۔ اور اپنے کپڑے پاک رکھو۔ اور گندگی سے دور رہو۔" (المدثر: ۱–۵)
🟢 Sahih Bukhari 4953 — Jabir ibn Abdullah on fatrat al-wahy 🟢 Quran 74:1–5 — Surat al-Muddaththir
✦ Reflection
He was frightened, covered, alone — and the command came: arise. The pauses in our lives do not disqualify us. They precede the call. The moment of our greatest vulnerability can be the moment of our greatest commissioning.
وہ ڈرے ہوئے، چادر میں لپٹے، اکیلے تھے — اور حکم آیا: اٹھو۔ زندگی کے وقفے ہمیں نا اہل نہیں بناتے — وہ دعوت سے پہلے آتے ہیں۔ سب سے بڑی کمزوری کا لمحہ سب سے بڑے مشن کا لمحہ بن سکتا ہے۔
02The Beginning of Da'wah — Secret and Open · بداية الدعوة
📅 ~610–613 CE · Years 1–4 of Prophethood
📍 Makkah · Dar al-Arqam
السابقون الأولون إلى الإسلام
The First Believers — One by One

The call began in the closest circles. The first to believe was Khadijah RA — without hesitation. The first youth was Ali ibn Abi Talib RA, approximately ten years old. The first freed man was Zayd ibn Harithah RA. The first free adult man from outside the family was Abu Bakr al-Siddiq RA. This sequence is preserved by Ibn Hisham from multiple chains (Sirat al-Nabawiyyah, 1:245–252). The Prophet ﷺ's own household believed first — those who knew him most intimately were the first convinced.

Abu Bakr RA then became the first to bring others into Islam through personal outreach. Through his standing among Quraysh, the following accepted Islam in the early secret period: Uthman ibn Affan, al-Zubayr ibn al-Awwam, Abd al-Rahman ibn Awf, Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas, and Talha ibn Ubaydillah — all among the ten given the glad tidings of Paradise. Ibn Hisham records this in detail (Sirat, 1:252–262). Their meeting place was the home of al-Arqam ibn Abi al-Arqam on the slope of Mount Safa — the first school of Islam.

دعوت قریبی حلقوں میں شروع ہوئی۔ پہلی ایمان لانے والی خدیجہ رضی اللہ عنہا — بغیر ایک لمحے کے تذبذب کے۔ پہلے نوجوان علی رضی اللہ عنہ، تقریباً دس سال کی عمر میں۔ پہلے آزاد بالغ مرد ابو بکر رضی اللہ عنہ۔ پھر ابو بکر رضی اللہ عنہ کی کوشش سے عثمان، زبیر، عبد الرحمٰن بن عوف، سعد بن ابی وقاص اور طلحہ رضی اللہ عنہم ایمان لائے — سب جنت کی بشارت پانے والوں میں سے۔ ارقم بن ابی الارقم کا گھر — صفا کی ڈھلان پر — اسلام کی پہلی درس گاہ بنا۔

🔵 Ibn Hisham, Sirat al-Nabawiyyah, 1:245–252 — order of first believers 🔵 Ibn Hisham, Sirat al-Nabawiyyah, 1:252–262 — Abu Bakr brings companions
✦ Reflection
The first Muslims came through personal trust — not compulsion, not spectacle. Those who knew him best believed first. Da'wah has never changed in its most essential form: one human being, truthfully, to another.
پہلے مسلمان ذاتی اعتماد کے ذریعے آئے — جبر سے نہیں۔ جو آپ ﷺ کو سب سے زیادہ جانتے تھے، پہلے ایمان لائے۔ دعوت اپنی اصل صورت میں کبھی نہیں بدلی: ایک انسان، سچائی کے ساتھ، دوسرے سے۔
03Public Da'wah — Mount Safa · الدعوة العلنية من جبل الصفا
📅 ~Year 4 of Prophethood · ~613 CE
📍 Makkah · Mount Safa
وَأَنذِرْ عَشِيرَتَكَ الْأَقْرَبِينَ
"Warn Your Nearest Kindred" — and Abu Lahab's Response

When the command came — "And warn your nearest kindred" (Quran 26:214) — the Prophet ﷺ climbed Mount Safa and called out in the Arab manner for urgent warning: "Ya sabahah!" The clans of Quraysh gathered — Banu Fihr, Banu Adi, all of them. He asked: "If I told you a cavalry was advancing from behind this mountain — would you believe me?" They said: "Yes — we have never known you to lie." He said: "Then I am a warner to you of a severe punishment ahead."

His uncle Abu Lahab — Abd al-Uzza ibn Abd al-Muttalib — leapt up and said: "Tabbanlak! May you perish for the rest of this day! Is this why you gathered us?" The gathering dissolved. Surat al-Masad (111) was then revealed — naming Abu Lahab and his wife by their deeds. Narrated by Ibn Abbas RA in Sahih Bukhari (4770).

جب آیت نازل ہوئی "اپنے قریبی رشتہ داروں کو ڈراؤ" — آپ ﷺ صفا پر چڑھے اور "یا صباحاہ!" کا نعرہ لگایا۔ قریش جمع ہوئے۔ آپ ﷺ نے فرمایا: "کیا مانو گے اگر کہوں فوج آ رہی ہے؟" سب نے کہا: ہاں — تم نے کبھی جھوٹ نہیں بولا۔ پھر تنبیہ دی۔ ابو لہب بھڑکا: "تبّاً لک! کیا اسی لیے جمع کیا؟" سورۃ المسد نازل ہوئی۔ (صحیح بخاری ۴۷۷۰)

📖 Quran · Surat al-Masad (111:1–5)
تَبَّتْ يَدَا أَبِي لَهَبٍ وَتَبَّ ﴿١﴾ مَا أَغْنَىٰ عَنْهُ مَالُهُ وَمَا كَسَبَ ﴿٢﴾ سَيَصْلَىٰ نَارًا ذَاتَ لَهَبٍ ﴿٣﴾ وَامْرَأَتُهُ حَمَّالَةَ الْحَطَبِ ﴿٤﴾ فِي جِيدِهَا حَبْلٌ مِّن مَّسَدٍ ﴿٥﴾
"May the hands of Abu Lahab be ruined, and ruined is he. His wealth will not avail him or that which he gained. He will enter a Fire of blazing flame — and his wife [as well], the carrier of firewood. Around her neck is a rope of twisted fiber." (Saheeh International · Surat al-Masad 111:1–5)
"ابو لہب کے ہاتھ ٹوٹ جائیں۔ اس کا مال کام نہ آیا۔ وہ بھڑکتی آگ میں داخل ہوگا — اور اس کی بیوی بھی، لکڑیاں اٹھانے والی۔ گردن میں کھجور کی رسی۔" (المسد: ۱–۵)
🟢 Sahih Bukhari 4770 — Ibn Abbas, Mount Safa public sermon 🟢 Quran 111:1–5 — Surat al-Masad
✦ Reflection
He built his first public call on his existing credibility — "you have never known me to lie." Character preceded the call. His first serious opponent was his own family. Prophethood does not exempt a person from rejection by those closest to them.
پہلا عوامی خطاب اپنی ساکھ پر کھڑا کیا — "تم نے مجھے کبھی جھوٹا نہیں پایا۔" کردار دعوت سے پہلے آتا ہے۔ اور پہلا سنگین مخالف اپنا چچا تھا — نبوت قریبیوں کے رد سے محفوظ نہیں رکھتی۔
04The Persecution — Bilal, Sumayyah, Ammar · تعذيب المسلمين
📅 ~613–615 CE
📍 Makkah — The Desert Outside
صَبْراً يَا آلَ يَاسِرٍ — فَإِنَّ مَوْعِدَكُمُ الْجَنَّةُ
Those Who Bore the Weight First

Bilal ibn Rabah RA — an Abyssinian slave owned by Umayya ibn Khalaf — was dragged to the scorching ground, laid on burning sand, and had a heavy boulder placed on his chest. The demand was: renounce Islam. His answer, every time: "Ahad! Ahad! — One! One!" — declaring the oneness of Allah. Ibn Hisham records this in the Sira (1:317–320) with strong classical transmission.

The family of Yasir ibn Amir suffered the deepest. His wife Sumayyah bint Khayyat RA was killed by Abu Jahl — the first martyr in the history of Islam. Her husband Yasir RA also died under torture. Their son Ammar RA survived. The Prophet ﷺ would pass by them in the desert and say: "Sabran ya ala Yasir — Patience, O family of Yasir! Your appointment is Paradise." Ibn Hisham records this (Sirat, 1:320), and al-Hakim graded the account Sahih.

Abu Bakr al-Siddiq RA used his own wealth to purchase and free enslaved believers — including Bilal RA. He also freed Khabbab ibn al-Aratt, Amir ibn Fuhayrah, and others. Ibn Hisham records this (Sirat, 1:318); Sahih Bukhari (3754) records the Prophet's ﷺ praise of Abu Bakr's spending for the sake of Allah.

بلال بن رباح رضی اللہ عنہ کو جلتی ریت پر لٹا کر سینے پر پتھر رکھا جاتا — وہ کہتے: "احد! احد!" سمیہ رضی اللہ عنہا — اسلام کی پہلی شہیدہ — ابو جہل کے ہاتھوں شہید ہوئیں۔ یاسر رضی اللہ عنہ بھی وفات پا گئے۔ آپ ﷺ فرماتے: "صبرًا یا آلَ یاسر — تمہارا وعدہ جنت ہے۔" ابو بکر رضی اللہ عنہ نے اپنے مال سے بلال رضی اللہ عنہ اور دیگر کو خرید کر آزاد کیا۔

🔵 Ibn Hisham, Sirat, 1:317–320 — Bilal's torture; strong transmission 🔵 Ibn Hisham, Sirat, 1:320 — Sumayyah RA first martyr; Prophet's words 🔵 Ibn Hisham, Sirat, 1:318 — Abu Bakr frees enslaved believers 🟢 Sahih Bukhari 3754 — Abu Bakr's spending praised 🟡 Sumayyah's martyrdom — strong classical chains; al-Hakim grades Sahih
✦ Reflection
"Ahad! Ahad!" — on a man's lips as the boulder crushes him. Faith is not only what you profess in safety — it is what you hold when every incentive says let go. Abu Bakr RA shows the other side: when you see injustice and you have resources, using them is also an act of faith.
"احد! احد!" — پتھر کے نیچے، جلتی ریت پر۔ ایمان صرف وہ نہیں جو سکون میں کہتے ہو — وہ بھی ہے جو اس وقت تھامتے ہو جب ہر چیز چھوڑنے کو کہے۔ ابو بکر رضی اللہ عنہ نے دوسرا سبق دیا: ظلم دیکھو اور جو ہے لگا دو — یہ بھی ایمان کا عمل ہے۔
05First Hijra — Migration to Abyssinia · الهجرة إلى الحبشة
📅 ~Year 5 of Prophethood · ~615 CE
📍 Makkah → Abyssinia (al-Habashah)
الهجرة إلى أرض الحبشة — أرض العدل
A Just King — and the Speech That Made Him Weep

As persecution intensified, the Prophet ﷺ said: "There is a king in Abyssinia who wrongs no one — go to him until Allah provides you relief." The first group was small. A larger group eventually numbered around eighty-three men and women. Quraysh sent two envoys — Amr ibn al-As and Abdullah ibn Abi Rabia — with gifts to the court of Ashamah al-Najashi (the Negus) to have the Muslims returned. Source: Ibn Hisham, Sirat, 1:321–344.

The Negus called both sides before his full court. The Muslim spokesman was Jafar ibn Abi Talib RA. He described their prior state of ignorance, then Islam's call to truth, then recited from Surat Maryam (19). The Negus wept until his beard was wet. His bishops wept. He said: "By God, the difference between what you say and what we say is no greater than this" — drawing a line on the ground. He refused to hand over the Muslims, returned Quraysh's gifts, and guaranteed them his protection. Source: Ibn Hisham, Sirat, 1:334–338.

جب مکہ میں ظلم ناقابل برداشت ہو گیا، آپ ﷺ نے فرمایا: "وہاں ایک بادشاہ ہے جو ظلم نہیں کرتا — اس کے پاس جاؤ۔" قریش کے ایلچی آئے — مگر جعفر بن ابی طالب رضی اللہ عنہ نے دربار میں سورۃ مریم تلاوت کی۔ نجاشی رو پڑا، اسقف رو پڑے۔ بادشاہ نے کہا: "ہمارے اور تمہارے کلام میں فرق بس اتنا ہے" — لکیر کھینچی۔ مسلمانوں کو پناہ دی، تحائف واپس کیے۔

🔵 Ibn Hisham, Sirat, 1:321–344 — full Abyssinia account 🔵 Ibn Hisham, Sirat, 1:334–338 — Jafar's speech; Negus's response
✦ Reflection
The first Muslim refuge was given by a Christian king — moved to tears by the Quran's account of Mary and Jesus. Truth resonates across divides, when presented honestly. The Negus — Ashamah, meaning "gift" — was exactly that.
پہلی مسلم پناہ گاہ ایک عیسائی بادشاہ نے دی — جو مریم اور عیسیٰ کے ذکر پر رو پڑا۔ سچائی مذہبی سرحدیں پار کرتی ہے۔ نجاشی — "اصحمہ" یعنی تحفہ — واقعی تحفہ تھا۔
06Aam al-Huzn — The Year of Sorrow · عام الحزن
📅 ~619 CE · Scholars differ on exact year
📍 Makkah · Taif
وفاة خديجة وأبي طالب — ورحلة الطائف
Khadijah RA — Abu Talib — Then the Stones of Taif

Within weeks of each other, the Prophet ﷺ lost his two greatest supporters. Khadijah bint Khuwaylid RA — his wife of twenty-five years, first believer, first comforter — passed away approximately 619 CE. Scholars differ on the precise date. Sahih Bukhari (3818) preserves the Prophet's ﷺ words about her virtue — he spoke of her with undimming love until the end of his life.

Shortly before or after — scholars differ on the order — his uncle Abu Talib also died. The Prophet ﷺ was at his bedside urging him until his final breath to say the shahada. Abu Talib died on the religion of his fathers. Sahih Bukhari (3884) records this painful moment. With his passing, the tribal shield that had protected the Prophet ﷺ from Quraysh for a decade was gone.

Now exposed, the Prophet ﷺ traveled to Taif — alone but for Zayd ibn Harithah RA — hoping the tribe of Thaqif would offer support. They refused contemptuously, then set their servants upon him with stones until his feet bled. He took shelter in a garden — and from that garden, wounded and alone, came the famous supplication recorded by Ibn Hisham (Sirat, 2:5–9).

📜 The Du'a of Taif — Ibn Hisham, Sirat al-Nabawiyyah, 2:5–9 (Classical Source)
"O Allah, I complain to You of my weakness, my scarcity of resources, and my humiliation before people. O Most Merciful of those who are merciful — You are the Lord of the weak, and You are my Lord. To whom will You entrust me? To a distant stranger who will mistreat me? Or to an enemy who will dominate me? If You are not angry with me, I do not care what I face — but Your wellbeing is more expansive for me. I take refuge in the light of Your face by which all darkness is dispelled... so that there be upon me neither Your anger nor Your wrath. To You belongs reproach until You are pleased. There is no power and no might except by You."

Source note: This supplication is recorded by Ibn Hisham (Sirat, 2:5–9) and is widely transmitted in Seerah literature. The exact wording is not found in Sahih Bukhari or Sahih Muslim — it is a classical Seerah source and is presented as such.

چند ہفتوں میں خدیجہ رضی اللہ عنہا اور ابو طالب — دونوں رخصت ہو گئے۔ آپ ﷺ طائف گئے مگر ٹھکرا دیے گئے، پتھروا کر نکالے گئے۔ لہولہان پاؤں لیے باغ کی دیوار کے سائے میں بیٹھے — اور وہ دعا کی: "اے اللہ! میں اپنی کمزوری کی تجھ سے شکایت کرتا ہوں... تیری رضا میرے لیے سب سے وسیع ہے۔" یہ دعا ابن ہشام ۲:۵ میں ہے — صحیح بخاری یا مسلم میں اس وضاحت کے ساتھ نہیں۔

🟢 Sahih Bukhari 3818 — Prophet's words about Khadijah RA 🟢 Sahih Bukhari 3884 — death of Abu Talib 🔵 Ibn Hisham, Sirat, 2:5–9 — Taif journey and du'a 🟡 Du'a of Taif — classical Seerah; exact wording not in Bukhari/Muslim ⚪ Order of deaths of Khadijah and Abu Talib — scholars differ
✦ Reflection
He named this year himself — Aam al-Huzn. And from its lowest point — a garden wall, bleeding, alone — came the most selfless prayer: not "destroy them," but "Your wellbeing is more expansive for me." He measured reality not by what had been done to him, but by whether Allah was pleased.
آپ ﷺ نے خود اسے "عام الحزن" کہا۔ اور اس سال کی سب سے نچلی جگہ سے — لہولہان، اکیلے — آئی سب سے بے غرض دعا: "تیری رضا میرے لیے سب سے وسیع ہے۔" آپ ﷺ نے دنیا کو اس سے نہیں ناپا کہ اس نے کیا کیا — بلکہ اس سے کہ اللہ راضی ہے یا نہیں۔
07Al-Isra wal-Miraj — The Night Journey · الإسراء والمعراج
📅 ~620 CE · Scholars differ on exact year
📍 Makkah → Jerusalem → The Heavens
سُبْحَانَ الَّذِي أَسْرَىٰ بِعَبْدِهِ لَيْلًا
Divine Comfort After Sorrow — The Gift of Five Prayers

After the Year of Sorrow — after loss, rejection, and exile — Allah honoured His Prophet ﷺ with an experience unprecedented in human history: the Isra, the night journey from Masjid al-Haram to Masjid al-Aqsa, then the Miraj, the ascension through the seven heavens. The Quran declares this in Surat al-Isra (17:1) as its primary source. The hadith account is in Sahih Bukhari (3887). The defining gift for the entire ummah was the five daily prayers — our inheritance from that night.

What matters here: this night came as divine comfort after the hardest year. When earth closed its doors, heaven opened its own.
📖 Quran · Surat al-Isra (17:1) — Primary Source
سُبْحَانَ الَّذِي أَسْرَىٰ بِعَبْدِهِ لَيْلًا مِّنَ الْمَسْجِدِ الْحَرَامِ إِلَى الْمَسْجِدِ الْأَقْصَى الَّذِي بَارَكْنَا حَوْلَهُ لِنُرِيَهُ مِنْ آيَاتِنَا ۚ إِنَّهُ هُوَ السَّمِيعُ الْبَصِيرُ
"Exalted is He who took His Servant by night from al-Masjid al-Haram to al-Masjid al-Aqsa, whose surroundings We have blessed, to show him of Our signs. Indeed, He is the Hearing, the Seeing." (Saheeh International · Surat al-Isra 17:1)
"پاک ہے وہ ذات جو اپنے بندے کو رات کے ایک حصے میں مسجد الحرام سے مسجد اقصیٰ تک لے گئی — تاکہ اسے اپنی نشانیاں دکھائے۔ بے شک وہی سننے والا اور دیکھنے والا ہے۔" (الاسراء: ۱)
🟢 Quran 17:1 — Surat al-Isra — primary source 🟢 Sahih Bukhari 3887 — Isra and Miraj account ⚪ Exact year — scholars differ (619–621 CE)
✦ Reflection
The pattern is consistent in the life of the Prophet ﷺ: the deepest valleys are followed by the highest ascents. Every time a door on earth closed, Allah opened something from heaven. The five prayers are proof that this night was real — we hold them five times every day.
نبی ﷺ کی زندگی میں یہ نمونہ بار بار ملتا ہے: گہری وادیوں کے بعد اونچی بلندیاں۔ جب بھی زمین کا دروازہ بند ہوا، آسمان نے اپنا کھولا۔ پانچ نمازیں — اس رات کی حقیقت کا ثبوت — دن میں پانچ بار ہمارے ہاتھ میں ہیں۔
08The Pledges of Aqabah — Dawn of Madinah · بيعة العقبة
📅 First: ~621 CE · Second: ~622 CE
📍 Mina · The Pass of Aqabah
بيعة العقبة الأولى والثانية
Twelve Men — Then Seventy-Five — and a Covenant Worth Paradise

During the Hajj season of approximately 621 CE, twelve men from Yathrib — from the tribes of Aws and Khazraj — met the Prophet ﷺ at the pass of Aqabah and pledged: not to associate partners with Allah, not to steal, not to commit adultery, not to kill their children, and not to disobey the Prophet ﷺ in what is right. This was the First Pledge of Aqabah. Source: Ibn Hisham, Sirat, 2:43–46. The Prophet ﷺ sent Mus'ab ibn Umayr RA with them — a young man of noble Qurayshi background who had given up luxury for Islam — to teach them the Quran.

The Second Pledge of Aqabah took place in 622 CE — secretly, in the dead of night, at the same pass. Seventy-three men and two women came. Their pledge: to protect the Prophet ﷺ as they protected their own women and children. Al-Abbas ibn Abd al-Muttalib — the Prophet's uncle, not yet a Muslim — attended to witness the seriousness of the contract and to ensure the delegation understood its gravity. He said: "If you cannot fulfil this, say so now." They replied: "We have heard, we understand, we accept." The Prophet ﷺ said: "I am of you and you are of me." Sources: Ibn Hisham, Sirat, 2:46–75; Sahih Bukhari 3892.

۶۲۱ء کے حج میں بارہ یثربی مردوں نے عقبہ پر بیعت کی — شرک نہیں، چوری نہیں، زنا نہیں، اولاد کو قتل نہیں۔ آپ ﷺ نے مصعب بن عمیر رضی اللہ عنہ کو ان کے ساتھ بھیجا۔ ایک سال میں یثرب بدل گیا۔ پھر ۶۲۲ء میں رات کے اندھیرے میں تہتر مرد اور دو خواتین آئے — عہد: آپ ﷺ کی حفاظت کریں گے جیسے اپنے عزیزوں کی۔ عباس بن عبد المطلب گواہ تھے۔ آپ ﷺ نے فرمایا: "میں تم میں سے ہوں اور تم مجھ میں سے ہو۔"

🔵 Ibn Hisham, Sirat, 2:43–46 — First Pledge of Aqabah 🔵 Ibn Hisham, Sirat, 2:46–75 — Second Pledge of Aqabah 🟢 Sahih Bukhari 3892 — the bay'ah
✦ Reflection
When Makkah would not have him, Madinah offered everything. The Ansar pledged their lives for a man most had never met. And Mus'ab ibn Umayr RA — who had worn the finest clothes in Makkah — changed an entire city, one household at a time, by teaching the Quran. Some of history's most significant transformations begin with one person, in one place, doing one thing faithfully.
مکہ نے نہ مانا، مدینے نے سب دے دیا۔ انصار نے جانیں لگا دیں ایک ایسے شخص کے لیے جنہیں زیادہ تر نے دیکھا بھی نہ تھا۔ مصعب بن عمیر رضی اللہ عنہ نے ایک انجان شہر میں ایک سال گزارا اور دنیا بدل دی۔ تاریخ کی سب سے بڑی تبدیلیاں ایک شخص، ایک جگہ، ایک کام — وفاداری سے کرنے سے شروع ہوتی ہیں۔
09Permission to Migrate — The Door Opens · إذن الهجرة
📅 ~622 CE · Before the Hijra
📍 Makkah al-Mukarramah
أُذِنَ لِلَّذِينَ يُقَاتَلُونَ بِأَنَّهُمْ ظُلِمُوا
The First Verse Permitting Resistance — and Abu Bakr Weeps with Joy

For twelve years in Makkah, the believers endured persecution without permission to defend themselves by force. The first divine permission came with Quran 22:39–40 — acknowledging that those being fought were wronged, driven from their homes for saying "Our Lord is Allah," and that resistance is now permitted. Scholars note this as one of the most significant legislative revelations — the first Quranic recognition of the right to self-defence.

With the pledges of Aqabah complete and Yathrib ready, the Prophet ﷺ received Allah's permission to migrate. He went to Abu Bakr al-Siddiq RA at an unusual hour — midday — and informed him. Abu Bakr RA had been waiting. He had kept two she-camels prepared for months. When the Prophet ﷺ said: "Allah has given permission for migration", Abu Bakr RA wept with joy — a weeping Aisha RA said she had never seen before — and asked: "Companionship, O Messenger of Allah?" The Prophet ﷺ replied: "Companionship." Source: Sahih Bukhari 3905, narrated by Aisha RA.

بارہ سال تک صبر — بغیر لڑنے کی اجازت کے۔ پھر قرآن کریم میں پہلی آیت نازل ہوئی جو مزاحمت کی اجازت دیتی ہے (الحج: ۳۹–۴۰): ان لوگوں کو اجازت دی گئی جن سے لڑائی ہو رہی ہے — کیونکہ ان پر ظلم ہوا۔ آپ ﷺ ابو بکر رضی اللہ عنہ کے گھر دوپہر میں آئے — غیر معمولی وقت۔ فرمایا: "ہجرت کی اجازت مل گئی۔" ابو بکر رضی اللہ عنہ خوشی سے رو پڑے — ایسا رونا جو عائشہ رضی اللہ عنہا نے پہلے نہیں دیکھا تھا — اور پوچھا: "ساتھ؟" آپ ﷺ نے فرمایا: "ساتھ۔" (صحیح بخاری ۳۹۰۵)

📖 Quran · Surat al-Hajj (22:39–40) — First Verse Permitting Resistance
أُذِنَ لِلَّذِينَ يُقَاتَلُونَ بِأَنَّهُمْ ظُلِمُوا ۚ وَإِنَّ اللَّهَ عَلَىٰ نَصْرِهِمْ لَقَدِيرٌ ﴿٣٩﴾ الَّذِينَ أُخْرِجُوا مِن دِيَارِهِم بِغَيْرِ حَقٍّ إِلَّا أَن يَقُولُوا رَبُّنَا اللَّهُ ﴿٤٠﴾
"Permission has been given to those who are being fought because they were wronged — and indeed, Allah is competent to give them victory. Those who were expelled from their homes without right — only because they said: Our Lord is Allah." (Saheeh International · Surat al-Hajj 22:39–40)
"اجازت دی گئی ان لوگوں کو جن سے لڑائی کی جا رہی ہے — کیونکہ ان پر ظلم ہوا — اور اللہ ان کی مدد پر قادر ہے۔ وہ جنہیں ان کے گھروں سے ناحق نکالا گیا — صرف اس لیے کہ انہوں نے کہا: ہمارا رب اللہ ہے۔" (الحج: ۳۹–۴۰)
📚 Sahih Bukhari 3905 — Abu Bakr RA Weeps with Joy (Sahih)
Aisha RA narrated: The Prophet ﷺ came to us at an unusual hour one day. Abu Bakr said: Something important must have happened. The Prophet ﷺ asked permission and entered. He said: "Tell everyone with you to leave." Abu Bakr said: "They are only family — my father and mother be your ransom, O Messenger of Allah." The Prophet ﷺ said: "I have been given permission to migrate." Abu Bakr said: "Companionship, O Messenger of Allah?" The Prophet ﷺ said: "Companionship." Aisha RA said: By Allah, before that day I did not know that anyone could weep with joy — until I saw Abu Bakr weep with joy on that day.
🟢 Quran 22:39–40 — first verse permitting resistance 🟢 Sahih Bukhari 3905 — Abu Bakr RA weeps with joy
✦ Reflection
Abu Bakr RA had kept two camels ready for months — quietly, faithfully, without knowing the day. This is the companion who truly believes: he prepares before the call, so that when it comes, he is already ready. The Hijra was not spontaneous. It was built on years of readiness, by people who believed enough to live prepared.
ابو بکر رضی اللہ عنہ نے مہینوں پہلے سے اونٹنیاں تیار کر رکھی تھیں — خاموشی سے، بغیر دن جانے۔ یہی حقیقی صحبت ہے: حکم آنے سے پہلے تیار رہنا۔ ہجرت اچانک نہیں تھی — یہ برسوں کی تیاری پر قائم تھی، ایسے لوگوں کی جو اتنا یقین رکھتے تھے کہ ہمیشہ تیار رہیں۔
