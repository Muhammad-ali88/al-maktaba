What Is Zakat?
ما هي الزكاة؟
The Word Itself — Two Meanings, One Act

The Arabic word زكاة carries two simultaneous meanings: purification and growth. When you give Zakat, two things happen at once: the wealth you give purifies the wealth that remains, and the act of giving causes your wealth — and your soul — to grow. This is not a tax. It is not charity in the ordinary sense. It is an act of worship through wealth, commanded 32 times in the Quran, almost always alongside prayer.

Zakat means cleaning and growing. Like washing a fruit — after washing, it is better. When you give 2.5 out of every 100, the remaining 97.5 becomes cleaner and more blessed.

زکاة کے دو معنی ہیں: پاکیزگی اور نشوونما۔ جب آپ زکاة دیتے ہیں، دو کام بیک وقت ہوتے ہیں: دیا گیا مال باقی مال کو پاک کرتا ہے اور دینے کا عمل آپ کے مال اور روح دونوں کو بڑھاتا ہے۔ قرآن میں 32 بار حکم دیا گیا، تقریباً ہمیشہ نماز کے ساتھ۔

The Quranic Foundation
الأساس القرآني
Quran 9:103 — The Command and Its Purpose
خُذْ مِنْ أَمْوَالِهِمْ صَدَقَةً تُطَهِّرُهُمْ وَتُزَكِّيهِم بِهَا وَصَلِّ عَلَيْهِمْ إِنَّ صَلَاتَكَ سَكَنٌ لَّهُمْ
"Take from their wealth a charity by which you purify them and cause them growth, and invoke blessings upon them."
Two explicit purposes: purification (tuthahirahum) and growth (wa tuzakkihim). Then: pray for those who give. Zakat is bidirectional — the giver gives wealth, the recipient gives dua.
دو صریح مقاصد: پاکیزگی اور نشوونما۔ پھر: ان کے لیے دعا کرو۔ زکاة دو طرفہ ہے — دینے والا مال دیتا ہے، لینے والا دعا دیتا ہے۔
Quran 2:43 — Always with Prayer
وَأَقِيمُوا الصَّلَاةَ وَآتُوا الزَّكَاةَ وَارْكَعُوا مَعَ الرَّاكِعِينَ
"Establish prayer and give Zakat and bow with those who bow."
One of 32 places where Salah and Zakat are commanded together. Prayer = your relationship with Allah. Zakat = your relationship with His creation. Neither substitutes the other.
ان 32 مقامات میں سے ایک جہاں نماز اور زکاة کا حکم ساتھ دیا گیا۔ نماز = اللہ سے تعلق۔ زکاة = اللہ کی مخلوق سے تعلق۔
⚠ The Warning — Quran 3:180
وَلَا يَحْسَبَنَّ الَّذِينَ يَبْخَلُونَ بِمَا آتَاهُمُ اللَّهُ مِن فَضْلِهِ هُوَ خَيْرًا لَّهُمْ بَلْ هُوَ شَرٌّ لَّهُمْ سَيُطَوَّقُونَ مَا بَخِلُوا بِهِ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ

"Let not those who withhold what Allah has given them think it is good for them. Rather it is evil. They will be encircled by what they withheld on the Day of Resurrection."

The Prophet ﷺ explained: whoever does not give Zakat — on the Day of Resurrection his wealth will become a serpent with two black spots that encircles his neck and bites him, saying: "I am your wealth, I am your treasure." Sahih
📚 Sahih Bukhari, Hadith 1403

The Prophetic Rules
التَّفصِيل النَّبَوِي
Three Conditions — All Three Required
ConditionMeaningSource
NisabWealth must reach the minimum threshold — 85g gold or 595g silverBukhari 1405
HawlWealth must have been above nisab for a full lunar year (~354 days)Abu Dawud 1573
Rate2.5% of total zakatable wealth after deductionsBukhari 1386
Silver Nisab (595g) — Default, Majority Contemporary ViewWorth significantly less than gold nisab, meaning more people are included in the obligation. Position of Sheikh Ibn Baz, Sheikh al-Qaradawi, most contemporary Zakat institutions.
Gold Nisab (85g) — Classical Scholarly PositionHigher threshold. Proponents argue it prevents Zakat becoming burdensome on modest savings. Classical fiqh preference.
Majority (Hanafi, Hanbali) — Zakat Due on All GoldNo exemption for jewellery in personal use. This is the calculator's default.
Minority (Shafi'i, Maliki) — Worn Jewellery May Be ExemptJewellery in regular personal use (not for investment) may be excluded. If you follow this position, exclude worn jewellery from your gold entry.
Zakat vs Sadaqah — Different Obligations
TermStatusAmount
Zakat — The Third PillarObligatory (Fard)2.5% on eligible wealth annually
Sadaqah — Voluntary CharityRecommended (Sunnah)Any amount, any time
ImportantSadaqah does not substitute for Zakat. Generous charitable giving throughout the year does not exempt you from calculating and paying Zakat. Zakat is a right the poor have over wealthy Muslims' wealth — not a gift.

📈 Investments & Funds سرمایہ کاری اور فنڈز 0 ›
Stocks, shares, mutual funds — current market valueحصص، میوچل فنڈز — موجودہ بازاری قدر2.5%
Use today's market value of all holdings. Scholars' mainstream position: Zakat is on the full current market value of shares, not just dividends. Ensure investments are in halal sectors.
آج کی کل بازاری قدر استعمال کریں۔ علماء کا اکثریتی موقف: زکاة حصص کی مکمل موجودہ قدر پر، نہ صرف منافع پر۔
Pension / Retirement funds — accessible amountپنشن فنڈ — قابل رسائی رقم2.5%
If you can access your pension now (or are of retirement age), include the full value. If locked, include only the vested/accessible portion. Contemporary scholars generally say Zakat is due when funds are accessible.
اگر ابھی رسائی ہے تو پوری قدر شامل کریں۔ اگر مقفل ہے تو صرف قابل رسائی حصہ۔ معاصر علماء عموماً کہتے ہیں زکاة رسائی پر واجب ہوتی ہے۔
Cryptocurrency — current total valueکریپٹو کرنسی — موجودہ کل قدر2.5%
Contemporary scholarly position: treat crypto held as an asset like investment wealth — 2.5% on current value after one lunar year above nisab. This is scholarly ijtihad on new matters — consult your scholar if unsure about specific coins.
معاصر علمی موقف: کریپٹو کو سرمایہ کاری جیسا سمجھیں — ایک قمری سال بعد موجودہ قدر پر 2.5%۔ یہ جدید معاملات پر اجتہاد ہے۔
🏪 Business & Property کاروبار اور جائیداد 0 ›
Business inventory — goods held for saleتجارتی سامان — فروخت کے لیے2.5%
Current market value of all goods you hold for sale. Exclude: equipment and tools used in the business (not for sale), your business premises.
فروخت کے لیے سامان کی موجودہ بازاری قدر۔ خارج کریں: کاروبار کا سازوسامان، احاطہ۔
Rental income received (net amount held)موصول کرایہ (موجود خالص رقم)2.5%
The rental income you have received and still hold. Zakat is on income collected, not the property value itself (unless the property is held for sale). Include only what remains after your expenses from that income.
موصول کردہ اور موجود کرایہ۔ زکاة آمدنی پر ہے، جائیداد کی قدر پر نہیں (جب تک فروخت کے لیے نہ ہو)۔
Property held for sale — current market valueفروخت کے لیے جائیداد — موجودہ بازاری قدر2.5%
Property you purchased specifically to sell. Your personal home, car, and personal items are NOT zakatable regardless of their value. Complex property situations: consult your scholar.
فروخت کے لیے خریدی جائیداد۔ آپ کا ذاتی گھر، گاڑی، ذاتی سامان — زکاة کے قابل نہیں۔ پیچیدہ حالات: عالم سے رجوع کریں۔
🤝 Money Others Owe You آپ کا دوسروں پر قرض 0 ›
Loans you have given to othersدوسروں کو دیے قرضے2.5%
⭐ Commonly missed. Money you lent is still your wealth — you are entitled to it. Include the amount you expect to receive back. If the borrower genuinely cannot repay, you may reduce this amount accordingly.
⭐ عام طور پر نظرانداز۔ دیا گیا قرض پھر بھی آپ کا مال ہے۔ واپسی متوقع رقم شامل کریں۔ اگر قرض دار واقعی ادا نہیں کر سکتا تو اس حساب سے کم کریں۔
Unpaid salary, invoices, or deposits owed to youادا نہ کی گئی تنخواہ، بل، یا ضمانتیں2.5%
Salary earned but not yet paid, outstanding invoices for work done, security deposits held by others that belong to you. Include only amounts you are confident of receiving.
کمائی گئی لیکن ابھی نہ ملی تنخواہ، کیے کام کے بقایا بل، دوسروں کے پاس آپ کی ضمانتیں۔ صرف وہ رقم جس کے ملنے کا یقین ہو۔
Step 3 — Deductions
Debts you owe othersدوسروں کے آپ پر قرضےReduces Zakat
Include: loans others have given you, credit card balances, immediate mortgage instalments due. Most scholars deduct only the immediately due portion of long-term loans, not the total outstanding mortgage balance.
شامل کریں: دوسروں کے دیے قرضے، کریڈٹ کارڈ بیلنس، فوری واجب قسطیں۔ اکثر علماء صرف فوری واجب حصہ کاٹتے ہیں، پوری بقایا رہن رقم نہیں۔
Bills immediately due (rent, tax, utilities)فوری واجب بلReduces Zakat
Bills due within the next few days or weeks only. Do not include future expenses not yet due — only what is immediately payable right now.
