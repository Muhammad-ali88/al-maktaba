Evening Adhkar — The Daily Armour

Just as the morning had its shield of remembrance, the evening had its counterpart — a comprehensive wrap of protection, gratitude, and forgiveness that closed the day's account. He ﷺ was consistent with both sets. The timing of evening adhkar: scholars differ slightly — most hold they begin after Asr and are best completed before or after Maghrib. This scholarly difference is noted where relevant below.

جس طرح صبح کے اذکار کا ایک حصار تھا، شام کے اذکار اس کا جوڑ تھے — حفاظت، شکر اور معافی کی ایک جامع چادر جو دن کا حساب بند کرتی تھی۔ آپ ﷺ دونوں پر یکساں پابند تھے۔ شام کے اذکار کا وقت: علماء میں معمولی اختلاف ہے — اکثر کا مؤقف ہے کہ یہ عصر کے بعد شروع ہوتے ہیں اور مغرب کے قریب یا بعد مکمل ہونے چاہیے۔

23a — Master Evening Dua · دعاء المساء
اللَّهُمَّ بِكَ أَمْسَيْنَا وَبِكَ أَصْبَحْنَا وَبِكَ نَحْيَا وَبِكَ نَمُوتُ وَإِلَيْكَ الْمَصِيرُ
Allahumma bika amsayna wa bika asbahna wa bika nahya wa bika namutu wa ilayka-l-masir
O Allah, by You we have reached the evening, and by You we reach the morning; by You we live and by You we die, and to You is the final destination.
اے اللہ، تیرے ہی ذریعے ہم نے شام کی اور تیرے ہی ذریعے صبح کریں گے؛ تیرے ہی ذریعے ہم جیتے ہیں اور تیرے ہی ذریعے مریں گے، اور تیری ہی طرف لوٹنا ہے۔
The evening mirror of the morning dua — surrendering the entire night: its waking, its sleep, its life, its death — all placed in Allah's hands before the world grows dark.
📚 Sunan Abu Dawud, Hadith 5068; Sunan al-Tirmidhi, Hadith 3391 — Hasan Sahih
23b — Ayat al-Kursi · آية الكرسي · Evening
اللَّهُ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ الْحَيُّ الْقَيُّومُ ۚ لَا تَأْخُذُهُ سِنَةٌ وَلَا نَوْمٌ ۚ لَهُ مَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَمَا فِي الْأَرْضِ ۗ مَنْ ذَا الَّذِي يَشْفَعُ عِنْدَهُ إِلَّا بِإِذْنِهِ ۚ يَعْلَمُ مَا بَيْنَ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَمَا خَلْفَهُمْ ۖ وَلَا يُحِيطُونَ بِشَيْءٍ مِنْ عِلْمِهِ إِلَّا بِمَا شَاءَ ۚ وَسِعَ كُرْسِيُّهُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ ۖ وَلَا يَئُودُهُ حِفْظُهُمَا ۚ وَهُوَ الْعَلِيُّ الْعَظِيمُ
Allah — there is no deity except Him, the Ever-Living, the Sustainer of existence. Neither drowsiness overtakes Him nor sleep. To Him belongs whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth. Who is it that can intercede with Him except by His permission? He knows what is before them and what will be after them, and they encompass not a thing of His knowledge except for what He wills. His Kursi extends over the heavens and the earth, and their preservation tires Him not. And He is the Most High, the Most Great. [Quran 2:255]
اللہ — اس کے سوا کوئی معبود نہیں، ہمیشہ زندہ، ہمیشہ قائم۔ نہ اسے اونگھ آتی ہے نہ نیند۔ آسمانوں اور زمین میں جو کچھ ہے اسی کا ہے۔ اس کی کرسی آسمانوں اور زمین پر محیط ہے۔ وہی بلند و برتر ہے۔
Recited once in the evening — provides Allah's protection until the following morning. The companion piece to its morning recitation, forming a complete daily shield.
📚 Sunan al-Nasai al-Kubra, Hadith 9928 — authenticated by Imam al-Albani: Sahih
23c — Three Quls · Evening · Three times each
قُلْ هُوَ اللَّهُ أَحَدٌ ۝ اللَّهُ الصَّمَدُ ۝ لَمْ يَلِدْ وَلَمْ يُولَدْ ۝ وَلَمْ يَكُن لَّهُ كُفُوًا أَحَدٌ
Say: He is Allah, the One. Allah, the Eternal Refuge. He neither begets nor is born. Nor is there to Him any equivalent. [Quran 112]
قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ الْفَلَقِ ۝ مِن شَرِّ مَا خَلَقَ ۝ وَمِن شَرِّ غَاسِقٍ إِذَا وَقَبَ ۝ وَمِن شَرِّ النَّفَّاثَاتِ فِي الْعُقَدِ ۝ وَمِن شَرِّ حَاسِدٍ إِذَا حَسَدَ
Say: I seek refuge in the Lord of the daybreak, from the evil of what He has created, from the evil of darkness when it settles, from the evil of the blowers in knots, from the evil of an envier when he envies. [Quran 113]
قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ النَّاسِ ۝ مَلِكِ النَّاسِ ۝ إِلَٰهِ النَّاسِ ۝ مِن شَرِّ الْوَسْوَاسِ الْخَنَّاسِ ۝ الَّذِي يُوَسْوِسُ فِي صُدُورِ النَّاسِ ۝ مِنَ الْجِنَّةِ وَالنَّاسِ
Say: I seek refuge in the Lord of mankind, the Sovereign of mankind, the God of mankind, from the evil of the retreating whisperer — who whispers in the breasts of mankind — from among jinn and mankind. [Quran 114]
یہ تینوں سورتیں شام کو تین تین بار — ہر شے سے کافی ہیں۔ سورۃ الاخلاص، سورۃ الفلق، سورۃ الناس۔
Recited three times each in the evening — "They are sufficient for you against everything." Complete protection for the night ahead.
📚 Sunan Abu Dawud, Hadith 5082; Sunan al-Tirmidhi, Hadith 3575 — Sahih
23d — Sayyid al-Istighfar · Evening · سيد الاستغفار
اللَّهُمَّ أَنْتَ رَبِّي لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ خَلَقْتَنِي وَأَنَا عَبْدُكَ وَأَنَا عَلَى عَهْدِكَ وَوَعْدِكَ مَا اسْتَطَعْتُ أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا صَنَعْتُ أَبُوءُ لَكَ بِنِعْمَتِكَ عَلَيَّ وَأَبُوءُ لَكَ بِذَنْبِي فَاغْفِرْ لِي فَإِنَّهُ لَا يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوبَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ
Allahumma anta rabbi la ilaha illa anta, khalaqtani wa ana 'abduka, wa ana 'ala 'ahdika wa wa'dika mastata'tu, a'udhu bika min sharri ma sana'tu, abu'u laka bini'matika 'alayya, wa abu'u laka bidhanbi, faghfir li fa-innahu la yaghfiru-dh-dhunuba illa ant
O Allah, You are my Lord, there is no god but You. You created me and I am Your servant, and I am upon Your covenant and promise as best I can. I seek refuge in You from the evil of what I have done. I acknowledge Your favour upon me and I acknowledge my sin, so forgive me, for none forgives sins but You.
اے اللہ، تو میرا رب ہے، تیرے سوا کوئی معبود نہیں۔ تو نے مجھے پیدا کیا اور میں تیرا بندہ ہوں۔ میں تجھ سے اپنے اعمال کے شر سے پناہ مانگتا ہوں۔ پس مجھے معاف کر دے کیونکہ تیرے سوا کوئی گناہ نہیں بخشتا۔
Said once with conviction in the evening — "whoever says it and dies before morning enters Paradise." The closing forgiveness of the day's account.
📚 Sahih Bukhari, Book of Invocations (Kitab al-Da'awat), Hadith 6306 — Sahih
23e — Evening Protection Dua · Three times
أَعُوذُ بِكَلِمَاتِ اللَّهِ التَّامَّاتِ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا خَلَقَ
A'udhu bi-kalimatillahi-t-tammati min sharri ma khalaq
I seek refuge in the perfect words of Allah from the evil of what He has created.
میں اللہ کے کامل کلمات کی پناہ میں آتا ہوں اس کی مخلوق کے شر سے۔
Said three times in the evening — "nothing will harm him until morning." One of the shortest and most powerful protection formulas in the Sunnah.
📚 Sahih Muslim, Book of Dhikr and Dua (Kitab al-Dhikr), Hadith 2708 — Sahih
📚 All five evening adhkar sourced individually above. Completeness of morning + evening adhkar: Sunan Abu Dawud, Hadith 1522 — Sahih — "The one who recites the morning and evening adhkar has fully fulfilled dhikr day and night."
◈ Why This Matters You end the day the way you began it — with gratitude, with reliance, with protection sought. The morning and evening adhkar together are a complete daily envelope. He ﷺ never left either end of the day without this.
🌿 Try Today Pick just one of these duas and say it after Maghrib tonight — start with 23e (three times, thirty seconds). Build from there.

Maghrib and Isha — His Evening Prayer Pattern

After Maghrib — Two Sunnah Rakats
Part of the twelve rawatib rakats (Practice 16) — the two after Maghrib were among his most consistently observed. These are prayed in the home according to his practice. (Sahih Muslim, Kitab Salat al-Musafirin, Hadith 728 — Sahih)
He Disliked Sleeping Before Isha · كَرَاهِيَةُ النَّوْمِ قَبْلَهَا
كَانَ يَكْرَهُ النَّوْمَ قَبْلَ الْعِشَاءِ وَالْحَدِيثَ بَعْدَهَا
Kana yakrahu-n-nawma qabla-l-'isha'i wa-l-hadithu ba'daha
He disliked sleeping before Isha and talking excessively after it.
📚 Sahih Bukhari, Book of the Times of Prayer (Kitab Mawaqit al-Salah), Hadith 568 — Sahih
After Isha — Two Sunnah Rakats
The final two rakats of the twelve rawatib — prayed after Isha, completing the day's voluntary architecture. (Sahih Muslim 728 — Sahih)

His evenings were not formless. Between Maghrib and Isha he was present with his family — engaged, warm, not withdrawn. He used this time for beneficial conversation, sometimes receiving companions and discussing matters of the community. After Isha he wound down — aware that the night had a higher purpose. He did not treat the hours before sleep as dead time to be filled. They were the transition toward tahajjud, and he treated them accordingly.

ان کی شامیں بے ترتیب نہیں تھیں۔ مغرب اور عشاء کے درمیان وہ اپنے اہل خانہ کے ساتھ موجود تھے — گرم جوش، حاضر۔ کبھی صحابہ آتے اور امور امت پر گفتگو ہوتی۔ عشاء کے بعد وہ سکون کی طرف آتے — جانتے ہوئے کہ رات کا ایک اعلیٰ مقصد ہے۔ سونے سے پہلے کے گھنٹوں کو بے مقصد نہیں جانے دیتے تھے۔

📚 Rawatib after Maghrib and Isha: Sahih Muslim, Hadith 728 — Sahih; Disliked sleeping before Isha: Sahih Bukhari, Hadith 568 — Sahih
◈ Why This Matters His evenings were structured — prayer, family, beneficial conversation, rest. There was no dead time. Every hour had a colour. The transition from Isha to sleep was itself a preparation for the night's most valuable act.
🌿 Try Today Pray the two sunnah rakats after Maghrib tonight. After Isha, choose beneficial rest over scrolling. Let the evening be preparation, not distraction.
✦ Sahih — Bukhari 🌙 After Isha PRACTICE 25

Limited Talk After Isha — The Discipline of the Night

كَانَ يَكْرَهُ النَّوْمَ قَبْلَ الْعِشَاءِ وَالْحَدِيثَ بَعْدَهَا
Kana yakrahu-n-nawma qabla-l-'isha'i wa-l-hadithu ba'daha
He disliked sleeping before Isha and [excessive] talking after it.
📋 Scholarly Clarification — Ibn Hajar (Fath al-Bari) This refers to excessive, purposeless talk — not all conversation. Ibn Hajar al-Asqalani notes in his commentary on this hadith that the Prophet ﷺ himself would speak after Isha with his wives, with companions, about knowledge and important matters. The prohibition is specifically against idle, time-wasting speech that drains the energy needed for Fajr and tahajjud.

This was one of the most quietly practical of his daily disciplines. He understood the architecture of the night: Isha was not the end of the day — it was the gateway to the night's most sacred time. Purposeless conversation, entertainment for its own sake, and idle interaction after Isha all have the effect of keeping the mind and body engaged when they should be winding down. His conversations after Isha — when he did have them — were always purposeful: learning, community matters, the affairs of his family. He knew that what you do in the hour before sleep shapes the quality of that sleep and, crucially, your ability to rise for tahajjud and Fajr.

یہ آپ ﷺ کے روزمرہ کے سب سے خاموش اور عملی ضبط نفس میں سے ایک تھا۔ آپ رات کے ڈھانچے کو سمجھتے تھے: عشاء دن کا اختتام نہیں تھا — یہ رات کے مقدس ترین وقت کا دروازہ تھا۔ بے مقصد گفتگو اور وقت ضائع کرنے والی بات چیت ذہن اور جسم کو اس وقت مشغول رکھتی ہے جب انہیں سکون کی طرف آنا چاہیے۔ آپ جانتے تھے کہ سونے سے پہلے کا گھنٹہ نیند کا معیار اور تہجد و فجر کے لیے اٹھنے کی صلاحیت کو متاثر کرتا ہے۔

📚 Sahih Bukhari, Book of the Times of Prayer (Kitab Mawaqit al-Salah), Hadith 568 — Sahih; Scholarly commentary: Fath al-Bari (Ibn Hajar al-Asqalani), explanation of Hadith 568
◈ Why This Matters What you do in the hour before sleep shapes its quality and your Fajr. He was not anti-social after Isha — he was intentional. The night had a purpose that idle talk threatened.
🌿 Try Today After Isha tonight — no social media scrolling, no idle group chats. Beneficial reading, dhikr, or early rest. Protect the gateway to your night.

✦ Sahih — Bukhari 🌙 Before Sleep PRACTICE 26

Wudu Before Sleep — Every Night

إِذَا أَتَيْتَ مَضْجَعَكَ فَتَوَضَّأْ وُضُوءَكَ لِلصَّلَاةِ
Idha atayta madja'aka fa-tawadda' wudu'aka lil-salah
"When you go to your bed, perform wudu as you do for prayer."

He ﷺ taught this as part of a comprehensive bedtime routine to al-Bara ibn Azib RA — personally, deliberately, with care. The full instruction covers: wudu, sleeping on the right side, the sleep dua. Wudu before sleep maintains ritual purity through the night — the body clean, the state one of readiness. He ﷺ remained in wudu consistently. Sleep in Islam is a form of death — the soul is taken and returned. He chose to meet this minor death in a state of purity, as one should wish to meet the final death. The angels of mercy do not leave a person who sleeps in a state of wudu — they continue to make dua for them throughout the night.

آپ ﷺ نے یہ حضرت براء بن عازب رضی اللہ عنہ کو سونے کے وقت کے ایک جامع معمول کے طور پر سکھایا — ذاتی طور پر، ارادے کے ساتھ۔ مکمل ہدایت میں شامل ہیں: وضو، دائیں کروٹ پر لیٹنا، سونے کی دعا۔ رات بھر طہارت برقرار رہتی ہے۔ نیند اسلام میں ایک چھوٹی موت ہے — آپ نے اس چھوٹی موت کو پاکیزگی کی حالت میں ملنا پسند کیا۔ رحمت کے فرشتے اس شخص سے نہیں جاتے جو وضو کی حالت میں سوتا ہے۔

📚 Sahih Bukhari, Book of Ablution (Kitab al-Wudu), Hadith 247 — Sahih (narrated by al-Bara ibn Azib RA)
◈ Why This Matters You don't know if you will wake. Sleeping in a state of wudu means you meet your Lord — if you do not wake — in that state. It also keeps the angels of mercy with you through the night.
🌿 Try Today Wudu before sleep tonight. Make it the last act before you lie down. Let the night begin in purity.
✦ Sahih — Bukhari 🌙 In Bed, Before Sleep PRACTICE 27

Ruqyah on Himself — The Three Quls Before Sleep

قُلْ هُوَ اللَّهُ أَحَدٌ ۝ اللَّهُ الصَّمَدُ ۝ لَمْ يَلِدْ وَلَمْ يُولَدْ ۝ وَلَمْ يَكُن لَّهُ كُفُوًا أَحَدٌ
Qul huwa Allahu ahad. Allahu-s-samad. Lam yalid wa lam yulad. Wa lam yakun lahu kufuwan ahad.
Say: He is Allah, the One. Allah, the Eternal Refuge. He neither begets nor is born. Nor is there to Him any equivalent. [Al-Ikhlas — 112]
قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ الْفَلَقِ ۝ مِن شَرِّ مَا خَلَقَ ۝ وَمِن شَرِّ غَاسِقٍ إِذَا وَقَبَ ۝ وَمِن شَرِّ النَّفَّاثَاتِ فِي الْعُقَدِ ۝ وَمِن شَرِّ حَاسِدٍ إِذَا حَسَدَ
Qul a'udhu bi-rabi-l-falaq. Min sharri ma khalaq. Wa min sharri ghasiqin idha waqab. Wa min sharri-n-naffathati fi-l-'uqad. Wa min sharri hasidin idha hasad.
Say: I seek refuge in the Lord of the daybreak, from the evil of what He has created, from the evil of darkness when it settles, from the evil of the blowers in knots, from the evil of an envier when he envies. [Al-Falaq — 113]
قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ النَّاسِ ۝ مَلِكِ النَّاسِ ۝ إِلَٰهِ النَّاسِ ۝ مِن شَرِّ الْوَسْوَاسِ الْخَنَّاسِ ۝ الَّذِي يُوَسْوِسُ فِي صُدُورِ النَّاسِ ۝ مِنَ الْجِنَّةِ وَالنَّاسِ
Qul a'udhu bi-rabbi-n-nas. Maliki-n-nas. Ilahi-n-nas. Min sharri-l-waswasi-l-khannas. Alladhi yuwaswisu fi suduri-n-nas. Mina-l-jinnati wa-n-nas.
Say: I seek refuge in the Lord of mankind, the Sovereign of mankind, the God of mankind, from the evil of the retreating whisperer — who whispers in the breasts of mankind — from among jinn and mankind. [An-Nas — 114]

Every night before sleep, he ﷺ would cup his hands, recite these three surahs, blow gently into his cupped palms, and then wipe his hands over his face and as much of his body as he could reach — doing this three times. Aisha RA described this practice with care and specificity. During his final illness, when he was too weak to perform the ruqyah on himself, she would do it for him — wiping his hands over his body with his own blessed hands. This nightly ruqyah was his personal spiritual armour against the vulnerabilities of the night — the fears, the dreams, the whispers that come in darkness. He faced them with Quran.

ہر رات سونے سے پہلے آپ ﷺ اپنی ہتھیلیاں جوڑتے، یہ تین سورتیں پڑھتے، ہتھیلیوں میں ہلکی پھونک مارتے، پھر چہرے اور جسم پر ہاتھ پھیرتے — تین بار۔ حضرت عائشہ رضی اللہ عنہا نے یہ عمل بڑی تفصیل سے بیان کیا۔ آپ کی آخری بیماری میں جب آپ خود نہیں کر سکتے تھے، حضرت عائشہ آپ کے ہاتھوں سے یہ کام کرتیں۔ یہ رات کی کمزوریوں کے خلاف آپ ﷺ کی ذاتی روحانی ڈھال تھی — اندھیرے کے خوف اور وسوسوں کا مقابلہ قرآن سے۔

📚 Sahih Bukhari, Book of Medicine (Kitab al-Tibb), Hadith 5017 — Sahih (narrated by Aisha RA, also Muslim)
◈ Why This Matters The night brings its own fears and vulnerabilities — anxieties, intrusive thoughts, the weight of the day carried into sleep. He faced them with Quran, not with distraction. The three surahs blow into cupped hands are his prescription.
🌿 Try Today In bed tonight: cup your hands, recite the three surahs, blow gently into your palms, wipe over your face. Three times. His personal nightly shield — now yours.

The Last Two Verses of Surah Baqarah — Before Sleep

آمَنَ الرَّسُولُ بِمَا أُنزِلَ إِلَيْهِ مِن رَّبِّهِ وَالْمُؤْمِنُونَ ۚ كُلٌّ آمَنَ بِاللَّهِ وَمَلَائِكَتِهِ وَكُتُبِهِ وَرُسُلِهِ لَا نُفَرِّقُ بَيْنَ أَحَدٍ مِّن رُّسُلِهِ ۚ وَقَالُوا سَمِعْنَا وَأَطَعْنَا ۖ غُفْرَانَكَ رَبَّنَا وَإِلَيْكَ الْمَصِيرُ
The Messenger has believed in what was revealed to him from his Lord, and so have the believers. All of them have believed in Allah and His angels and His books and His messengers, saying: "We make no distinction between any of His messengers." And they say: "We hear and we obey. Your forgiveness, our Lord, and to You is the final destination." [Quran 2:285]
لَا يُكَلِّفُ اللَّهُ نَفْسًا إِلَّا وُسْعَهَا ۚ لَهَا مَا كَسَبَتْ وَعَلَيْهَا مَا اكْتَسَبَتْ ۗ رَبَّنَا لَا تُؤَاخِذْنَا إِن نَّسِينَا أَوْ أَخْطَأْنَا ۚ رَبَّنَا وَلَا تَحْمِلْ عَلَيْنَا إِصْرًا كَمَا حَمَلْتَهُ عَلَى الَّذِينَ مِن قَبْلِنَا ۚ رَبَّنَا وَلَا تُحَمِّلْنَا مَا لَا طَاقَةَ لَنَا بِهِ ۖ وَاعْفُ عَنَّا وَاغْفِرْ لَنَا وَارْحَمْنَا ۚ أَنتَ مَوْلَانَا فَانصُرْنَا عَلَى الْقَوْمِ الْكَافِرِينَ
Allah does not burden a soul beyond that it can bear. It will have the reward of what it earned, and it will bear the consequence of what it did. Our Lord, do not impose blame upon us if we forget or make an error. Our Lord, and lay not upon us a burden like that which You laid upon those before us. Our Lord, and burden us not with that which we have no ability to bear. And pardon us; and forgive us; and have mercy upon us. You are our protector, so give us victory over the disbelieving people. [Quran 2:286]

These two ayahs are among the most complete in the entire Quran. The first is a creed — a declaration of comprehensive faith by the Prophet and the believers, ending in surrender: "We hear and we obey." The second is the most beautiful comprehensive dua in the scripture — beginning with the most comforting truth (Allah does not burden a soul beyond its capacity) and ending with seven requests: no blame for forgetfulness, no burden like those before, no unbearable burden, pardoning, forgiveness, mercy, and victory. He ﷺ said they will "suffice" whoever recites them at night — scholars understand this as both spiritual protection and the settlement of the night's account before sleep.

یہ دو آیات پورے قرآن میں سب سے جامع آیات میں سے ہیں۔ پہلی ایمان کا اقرار ہے — سمعنا واطعنا۔ دوسری کتاب میں سب سے خوبصورت جامع دعا ہے — سب سے تسلی بخش حقیقت سے شروع ہوتی ہے (اللہ کسی کو اس کی طاقت سے زیادہ بوجھ نہیں دیتا) اور سات درخواستوں پر ختم ہوتی ہے۔ آپ ﷺ نے فرمایا: یہ دو آیات رات کو پڑھنے والے کو کافی ہوں گی۔

📚 Sahih Bukhari, Book of the Virtues of the Quran (Kitab Fada'il al-Quran), Hadith 5009; Sahih Muslim, Book of Prayer of Travellers, Hadith 807 — Sahih
◈ Why This Matters End your day with the most complete statement of faith and the most beautiful plea for mercy in the Quran. "Allah does not burden a soul beyond that it can bear" — let that be the last theological truth you carry into sleep.
🌿 Try Today Recite these two ayahs before you close your eyes tonight. Read the translation first — carry the meaning with you into sleep, not just the words.
✦ Sahih / Hasan Sahih — Bukhari & Abu Dawud 🌙 Last Words Before Sleep PRACTICE 29

The Sleep Dua — Last Words Before Closing the Eyes

بِاسْمِكَ اللَّهُمَّ أَمُوتُ وَأَحْيَا
Bismika Allahumma amutu wa ahya
In Your name, O Allah, I die and I live.
اللَّهُمَّ قِنِي عَذَابَكَ يَوْمَ تَبْعَثُ عِبَادَكَ
Allahumma qini adhabaka yawma tab'athu ibadak
O Allah, protect me from Your punishment on the Day You resurrect Your servants.

These were his last words before sleep — not a mechanical recitation but a theological statement. "In Your name I die and I live" — not poetry, but truth. Sleep is a taking of the soul. He was acknowledging, at the threshold of unconsciousness, that his life and his death were both in Allah's name. Every night he made this surrender explicit. He also paired this with the dua asking for protection from punishment on the Day of Resurrection — connecting the small death of sleep to the great death ahead. Both duas together are a complete orientation: the soul surrendered to Allah in the name of Allah, asking for protection until the final account.

یہ ان کے سونے سے پہلے کے آخری الفاظ تھے — کوئی رسمی ورد نہیں بلکہ ایک عقیدی بیان۔ "تیرے نام پر مرتا اور جیتا ہوں" — شاعری نہیں، سچائی۔ نیند روح کا قبض کیا جانا ہے۔ آپ ﷺ لاشعوری کی دہلیز پر یہ اقرار کرتے تھے کہ ان کی زندگی اور موت دونوں اللہ کے نام پر ہیں۔ ہر رات انہوں نے یہ تسلیم صراحت سے کی۔

📚 Sleep dua (amutu wa ahya): Sahih Bukhari, Book of Invocations (Kitab al-Da'awat), Hadith 6311, Hadith 6324 — Sahih; Dua for protection from punishment: Sunan Abu Dawud, Hadith 5045; Sunan al-Tirmidhi, Hadith 3401 — Hasan Sahih
◈ Why This Matters What are your last words before sleep? He chose surrender — complete, explicit, theological surrender. "In Your name I die and I live" is the most honest thing a person can say at the edge of sleep.
🌿 Try Today Make "Bismika Allahumma amutu wa ahya" your literal last words before sleep — after everything else, after the phone is down, after the light is off. Let surrender be the last conscious act.
◈ Sahih / Hasan Sahih — Bukhari & Abu Dawud 🌙 Sleeping Position PRACTICE 30

Sleeping on the Right Side — Even Sleep Had Sunnah

اجْعَلْ يَمِينَكَ تَحْتَ خَدِّكَ الأَيْمَنِ
Ij'al yaminaka tahta khaddika-l-ayman
"Place your right hand under your right cheek."

His sleeping position was specific and documented: on his right side, right hand placed under his right cheek. Multiple narrations confirm this as his established practice. It is part of the same comprehensive bedtime routine taught to al-Bara ibn Azib RA — wudu, right side, sleep dua. The right side carries consistent significance across the Sunnah: he used his right hand for eating, for greeting, for giving. Even in sleep, his body maintained a deliberate, sunnah orientation. Modern cardiac research independently notes that sleeping on the right side is optimal for heart position — the heart sits slightly toward the left in the chest, and right-side sleeping reduces compression on it.

آپ ﷺ کی سونے کی پوزیشن مخصوص اور دستاویزی ہے: دائیں کروٹ پر، دایاں ہاتھ دائیں گال کے نیچے۔ متعدد روایات اسے آپ کا مستقل عمل ثابت کرتی ہیں۔ یہ حضرت براء بن عازب کو سکھائے گئے جامع سونے کے معمول کا حصہ ہے۔ دائیں جانب پوری سنت میں مستقل اہمیت رکھتا ہے — کھانے، سلام کرنے، دینے میں۔ حتی کہ نیند میں بھی آپ ﷺ کے جسم نے ایک ارادی، سنت رخ اختیار کیا۔

📚 Sahih Bukhari, Book of Invocations, Hadith 6311 — Sahih; Right hand under cheek: Sunan Abu Dawud, Hadith 5045 — Hasan Sahih
◈ Why This Matters Even his sleep had sunnah — every moment of the day and night was a potential act of worship. The right side is not just tradition; it is a deliberate orientation that runs through the entire fabric of his practice.
🌿 Try Today Right side. Right hand under right cheek. Before you do anything else in bed — make this physical intention. It takes one second and completes the bedtime sunnah.

✦ Sahih — Bukhari & Muslim 🌟 Last Third of the Night PRACTICE 31

Tahajjud — The Prayer He Never Abandoned

أَفَلَا أَكُونُ عَبْدًا شَكُورًا
Afala akunu 'abdan shakura
"Should I not be a grateful servant?"

Aisha RA asked him ﷺ: "Why do you do this when Allah has forgiven your past and future sins?" This was his answer.

He Prayed Until His Feet Swelled — Sahih Bukhari 1130
Aisha RA: "He ﷺ would pray at night until his feet became swollen. I said: 'O Messenger of Allah, why do you do this when Allah has already forgiven your past and future sins?' He said: 'Should I not be a grateful servant?'" (Sahih Bukhari, Book of Tahajjud, Hadith 1130; Sahih Muslim, Hadith 2819 — Sahih)
The Best Prayer After the Obligatory · أفضل الصلاة
أَفْضَلُ الصَّلَاةِ بَعْدَ الْفَرِيضَةِ صَلَاةُ اللَّيْلِ
Afdalu-s-salati ba'da-l-farida salatu-l-layl
"The best prayer after the obligatory is the night prayer."
📚 Sahih Muslim, Book of Fasting (Kitab al-Siyam), Hadith 1163 — Sahih
The Divine Descent — Last Third of the Night · النزول الإلهي
يَنْزِلُ رَبُّنَا تَبَارَكَ وَتَعَالَى كُلَّ لَيْلَةٍ إِلَى السَّمَاءِ الدُّنْيَا حِينَ يَبْقَى ثُلُثُ اللَّيْلِ الْآخِرُ فَيَقُولُ مَنْ يَدْعُونِي فَأَسْتَجِيبَ لَهُ مَنْ يَسْأَلُنِي فَأُعْطِيَهُ مَنْ يَسْتَغْفِرُنِي فَأَغْفِرَ لَهُ
Yanzilu rabbuna tabaraka wa ta'ala kulla laylatin ila-s-sama'i-d-dunya hina yabqa thuluthu-l-layli-l-akhiru fa-yaqulu: man yad'uni fa-astajiba lah, man yas'aluni fa-u'tiyah, man yastaghfiruni fa-aghfira lah
"Our Lord descends to the lowest heaven in the last third of every night and says: Who is calling upon Me that I may answer? Who is asking of Me that I may give? Who is seeking My forgiveness that I may forgive?"
📚 Sahih Bukhari, Book of Tahajjud (Kitab al-Tahajjud), Hadith 1145; Sahih Muslim, Book of Prayer of Travellers, Hadith 758 — Sahih
How He Prayed — Number of Rakats · عدد الركعات
Aisha RA: "He ﷺ never exceeded eleven rakats in Ramadan or other months." His typical pattern: 8 rakats of tahajjud + 3 Witr. (Sahih Bukhari, Book of Tahajjud, Hadith 1147 — Sahih) The minimum is two rakats — he said: "The night prayer is two rakats by two rakats." (Sahih Bukhari 990 — Sahih)
📋 Scholarly Note — Obligatory for the Prophet ﷺ, Recommended for the Ummah Tahajjud was specifically obligatory for the Prophet ﷺ (Quran 73:2–4). For the ummah it is the most recommended voluntary prayer — not obligatory. Scholars across all schools of fiqh are unanimous that it was his most consistent practice beyond the five obligatory prayers and the most recommended voluntary act of worship for believers.

Tahajjud was not an "extra" for him ﷺ — it was the foundation. He prayed it every night of his adult life: in Makkah when he was persecuted, in Madinah when he was statesman and prophet simultaneously, while travelling, during illness, in times of grief and in times of joy. It was where the Quran was first revealed — in the quiet of the night. It was the prayer of every prophet before him. It is where the deepest intimacy with Allah is accessible in this life. He said the closest you are to Allah is in the last third of the night — and he was there, every night, standing on swollen feet out of gratitude for an already-forgiven soul. He did not pray tahajjud because he needed to. He prayed it because he wanted to.

تہجد آپ ﷺ کے لیے "اضافی" نہیں تھی — یہ بنیاد تھی۔ آپ نے اپنی پوری بالغ زندگی میں ہر رات یہ نماز ادا کی: مکہ میں جب ستائے جاتے تھے، مدینہ میں جب بیک وقت رہنما اور نبی تھے، سفر میں، بیماری میں، غم اور خوشی دونوں میں۔ یہیں قرآن نازل ہوا — رات کی خاموشی میں۔ یہ ان تمام انبیاء کی نماز تھی جو آپ سے پہلے آئے۔ آپ نے تہجد اس لیے نہیں پڑھی کہ ضرورت تھی۔ آپ نے اس لیے پڑھی کیونکہ چاہتے تھے۔

📚 Feet swollen: Sahih Bukhari, Kitab al-Tahajjud, Hadith 1130; Muslim 2819 — Sahih | Best prayer: Muslim 1163 — Sahih | Divine descent: Bukhari 1145, Muslim 758 — Sahih | 11 rakats: Bukhari 1147 — Sahih | Quran 17:79; 73:2–4
◈ Why This Matters Every prophet was known for their night prayer. Every wali is described by their tahajjud. The last third of the night is when Allah asks: "Who is calling upon Me?" — and He is already there, already descending, already asking. The question is only whether we are awake.
🌿 Try Today Set an alarm for 30 minutes before Fajr. Pray just two rakats. Make your dua in sujood. Begin tonight — not next week, not in Ramadan. Tonight. The Lord descends every night.
✦ Sahih — Bukhari & Muslim 🌟 After Tahajjud — Before Fajr PRACTICE 32

Witr — The Seal of the Night Prayer

اجْعَلُوا آخِرَ صَلَاتِكُمْ بِاللَّيْلِ وِتْرًا
Ij'alu akhira salatikum bi-l-layli witran
"Make Witr the last of your night prayer."
اللَّهُمَّ اهْدِنِي فِيمَنْ هَدَيْتَ وَعَافِنِي فِيمَنْ عَافَيْتَ وَتَوَلَّنِي فِيمَنْ تَوَلَّيْتَ وَبَارِكْ لِي فِيمَا أَعْطَيْتَ وَقِنِي شَرَّ مَا قَضَيْتَ فَإِنَّكَ تَقْضِي وَلَا يُقْضَى عَلَيْكَ وَإِنَّهُ لَا يَذِلُّ مَنْ وَالَيْتَ وَلَا يَعِزُّ مَنْ عَادَيْتَ تَبَارَكْتَ رَبَّنَا وَتَعَالَيْتَ
Allahumma-hdini fiman hadayt, wa 'afini fiman 'afayt, wa tawallani fiman tawallayt, wa barik li fima a'tayt, wa qini sharra ma qadayt — fa-innaka taqdi wa la yuqda 'alayk, wa innahu la yadhillu man walayt, wa la ya'izzu man 'adayt, tabarakta rabbana wa ta'alayt
O Allah, guide me among those You have guided, grant me wellbeing among those You have granted wellbeing, take me as an ally among those You have taken as allies, bless me in what You have given, and protect me from the evil of what You have decreed — for You decree and none decrees against You. Indeed, he is not humiliated whom You befriend, nor is he honoured whom You oppose. Blessed are You, our Lord, and Exalted.

Witr — the odd-numbered prayer that seals the night — was something he ﷺ never abandoned, whether at home or travelling. He commanded the believers to make it the last prayer of the night. The number of rakats: minimum one, his practice was typically three. He taught the dua of Qunoot above to his grandson al-Hasan ibn Ali RA to recite in Witr. Witr is the mathematical completion of the night's worship — the even made odd, the account formally closed. He would not leave the night unsealed. Every night of tahajjud ended with Witr, and the night was complete.

وتر — طاق رکعت والی نماز جو رات کو مہر کرتی ہے — آپ ﷺ نے گھر ہو یا سفر، کبھی نہیں چھوڑی۔ آپ نے اسے رات کی آخری نماز بنانے کا حکم دیا۔ رکعتوں کی تعداد: کم از کم ایک، آپ کا عمل عموماً تین تھا۔ آپ نے یہ دعائے قنوت اپنے نواسے حضرت حسن بن علی رضی اللہ عنہ کو وتر میں پڑھنے کے لیے سکھائی۔ تہجد کی ہر رات وتر پر ختم ہوتی اور رات مکمل ہو جاتی۔

📚 Witr as last prayer: Sahih Bukhari, Book of Witr (Kitab al-Witr), Hadith 998; Sahih Muslim, Hadith 751 — Sahih | Never abandoned: Sahih Bukhari, Hadith 999 — Sahih | Qunoot: Sunan Abu Dawud, Hadith 1425; Sunan al-Tirmidhi, Hadith 464 — Hasan Sahih
◈ Why This Matters End your night prayer as he ﷺ ended his — with Witr, with the Qunoot dua, with the formal sealing of the night's worship. Even if you cannot pray tahajjud tonight, pray at least Witr. The night should not be left unsealed.
🌿 Try Today If you cannot pray tahajjud tonight — pray at minimum one rakat of Witr before sleep. Recite the Qunoot dua above. Seal your night.

His Character at Home — The Hidden Sunnah

كَانَ يَكُونُ فِي مِهْنَةِ أَهْلِهِ فَإِذَا حَضَرَتِ الصَّلَاةُ خَرَجَ إِلَى الصَّلَاةِ
Kana yakunu fi mihnati ahlihi — fa-idha hadarat-is-salatu kharaja ila-s-salah
"He would be helping his family with their work — and when the time for prayer came, he would go out to prayer."

Aisha RA was asked: "What did the Prophet ﷺ do at home?" This was her answer. She also said: "He would sew his sandals, patch his clothes, and help with housework — just as any of you would help in his home." (Sahih Bukhari, Book of Good Manners, Hadith 676 — Sahih)

He was the Messenger of Allah, the leader of the first Muslim community, the recipient of revelation — and he sewed his own sandals, patched his own clothes, milked his own goats, and helped in the home. There was no gap between who he was in the masjid and who he was at home. Aisha RA lived with him — she would have known if there was any difference between the public prophet and the private person. There was none. His home was not the place where the mask came off; it was where the same person continued, in a smaller, more intimate register. The domestic was not beneath him. It was part of his sunnah — perhaps the most personal and most difficult to imitate of all.

آپ اللہ کے رسول تھے، پہلی مسلم جماعت کے رہنما، وحی کے وصول کنندہ — اور آپ اپنے جوتے خود سیتے تھے، کپڑے خود پیوند کرتے تھے، بکریاں خود دوہتے تھے، گھر کے کام میں ہاتھ بٹاتے تھے۔ مسجد کے آپ ﷺ اور گھر کے آپ ﷺ میں کوئی فرق نہیں تھا۔ حضرت عائشہ رضی اللہ عنہا ان کے ساتھ رہتی تھیں — اگر کوئی فرق ہوتا تو انہیں ضرور معلوم ہوتا۔ کوئی فرق نہیں تھا۔ گھر کا کام ان کے لیے کمتر نہیں تھا۔ یہ ان کی سنت کا حصہ تھا۔

📚 Sahih Bukhari, Book of Good Manners (Kitab al-Adab), Hadith 676 — Sahih (narrated by Aisha RA via al-Aswad ibn Yazid)
◈ Why This Matters Spirituality that only exists in the masjid is incomplete. His sunnah was total — it ran through the prayer hall and the kitchen with equal integrity. The person who prays tahajjud but does not help at home has not yet fully followed him ﷺ.
🌿 Try Today Do one household task today that you would normally leave for someone else. Wash the dishes. Make the bed. Fold the laundry. His sunnah is waiting in the ordinary.
✦ Sahih — Muslim / Bukhari ✨ Every Friday PRACTICE 34

Friday — The Weekly Eid

خَيْرُ يَوْمٍ طَلَعَتْ عَلَيْهِ الشَّمْسُ يَوْمُ الْجُمُعَةِ
Khayru yawmin tala'at 'alayhi-sh-shamsu yawmu-l-jumu'ah
"Friday is the best day the sun rises upon."
34a — Surah Al-Kahf on Friday · سورة الكهف
"Whoever reads Surah Al-Kahf on Friday, a light will shine for him between the two Fridays." (Mustadrak al-Hakim 2:399; authenticated by Imam al-Albani — Sahih)
34b — Abundant Salawat · الصلاة على النبي ﷺ
اللَّهُمَّ صَلِّ عَلَى مُحَمَّدٍ وَعَلَى آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ
"Send abundant salawat on me on Friday, for your salawat is presented to me." (Sunan Abu Dawud, Hadith 1047; Sunan Ibn Majah, Hadith 1636 — Sahih)
34c — Ghusl on Friday · الغسل
"The ghusl on Friday is obligatory upon every adult." (Sahih Bukhari, Book of Friday, Hadith 877 — Sahih)
📋 Scholarly Note The Hanbali school holds Friday ghusl as wajib (obligatory). The Shafi'i, Maliki, and Hanafi schools hold it as sunnah mu'akkadah (strongly recommended). This difference is well-documented. All schools agree it is one of the most emphasised sunnahs of the week.
34d — The Hidden Hour of Acceptance · ساعة الإجابة
"There is an hour on Friday in which, if a Muslim is standing in prayer and asks Allah for something, He gives it to him." — Scholars say: most likely the last hour before Maghrib on Friday. Be present in dua at that time. (Sahih Bukhari, Book of Friday, Hadith 935; Sahih Muslim, Book of Friday, Hadith 852 — Sahih)

Friday was the weekly Eid — not in the sense of celebration alone but in the sense of distinct spiritual elevation. Its practices are specific, sourced, and layered: ghusl to prepare the body, Surah Al-Kahf to light the week ahead, salawat to connect with the Prophet ﷺ whose salawat are presented to him, and the hidden hour of acceptance to close it with dua. The Prophet ﷺ elevated Friday beyond the obligation of Jumu'ah — the day itself was to be treated as the best day of the week in every practice.

جمعہ ہفتہ وار عید تھی — صرف خوشی کے معنی میں نہیں بلکہ روحانی بلندی کے لحاظ سے۔ اس کے اعمال مخصوص، مستند اور تہہ دار ہیں: جسم کی تیاری کے لیے غسل، آنے والے ہفتے کو روشن کرنے کے لیے سورۃ الکہف، نبی ﷺ سے تعلق کے لیے درود، اور قبولیت کی پوشیدہ گھڑی کو دعا سے بند کرنا۔

📚 Best day: Sahih Muslim, Book of Friday (Kitab al-Jumu'ah), Hadith 854 — Sahih | Al-Kahf: al-Hakim 2:399 — Sahih (al-Albani) | Salawat: Abu Dawud 1047, Ibn Majah 1636 — Sahih | Ghusl: Bukhari 877 — Sahih | Hidden hour: Bukhari 935, Muslim 852 — Sahih
◈ Why This Matters Friday is the highest day of the week — not by cultural tradition but by prophetic designation. Its practices are complete, specific, and accessible. Each one takes minutes. Together they mark the week's spiritual peak.
🌿 Try Today (This Friday) Begin with ghusl. Read Surah Al-Kahf — even in parts. Increase salawat. Before Maghrib on Friday: stand in dua. Let the best day of the week be treated as such.
✦ Sahih — Muslim ✨ Monday & Thursday PRACTICE 35

Monday and Thursday Fasting — Presenting Deeds in the Best State

ذَانِكَ يَوْمَانِ تُعْرَضُ فِيهِمَا الْأَعْمَالُ عَلَى رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ فَأُحِبُّ أَنْ يُعْرَضَ عَمَلِي وَأَنَا صَائِمٌ
Dhanika yawmani tu'radu fihima-l-a'malu 'ala rabbi-l-'alamin, fa-uhibbu an yu'rada 'amali wa ana sa'im
"Those are two days on which deeds are presented to the Lord of the worlds, and I love that my deeds are presented while I am fasting."

This was his most consistent voluntary fast — not Ramadan, which is obligatory, but the weekly Monday and Thursday fast that he maintained throughout his life. He was asked why he observed these two days specifically, and his answer was both simple and profound: deeds are presented to Allah on Monday and Thursday, and he wanted his deeds to reach Allah while he was fasting. He was also asked about Monday specifically, and he said: "That is the day I was born, the day I received revelation, and the day I died — and I love to fast on it." (Sahih Muslim 1162). The fast was his way of managing the presentation of his life's work to his Lord.

یہ آپ ﷺ کا سب سے مستقل نفل روزہ تھا — رمضان نہیں جو فرض ہے، بلکہ ہفتہ وار پیر اور جمعرات کا روزہ جو آپ نے پوری زندگی رکھا۔ آپ ﷺ سے ان دو دنوں کی وجہ پوچھی گئی تو آپ نے سادہ اور گہرا جواب دیا: پیر اور جمعرات کو اعمال اللہ کے سامنے پیش ہوتے ہیں، اور آپ چاہتے تھے کہ ان کے اعمال روزے کی حالت میں پیش ہوں۔ پیر کے بارے میں آپ نے فرمایا: "یہ وہ دن ہے جس میں میری ولادت ہوئی، مجھ پر وحی نازل ہوئی اور میں نے وفات پائی۔"

📚 Sahih Muslim, Book of Fasting (Kitab al-Siyam), Hadith 1162 — Sahih; Monday's significance: Sahih Muslim 1162 — Sahih
◈ Why This Matters You can choose what state your deeds are in when they are presented to Allah. He chose fasting — the act of restraint and gratitude that prepares the soul. Monday and Thursday come every week. The choice is weekly.
🌿 Try Today Try fasting next Monday or Thursday. Start with one day — begin with the intention tonight. His practice was both days; your beginning is one.
